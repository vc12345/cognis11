Module 48: Non-Standard Net Generalization

Phase 1: The X-Ray

Irregular solids follow the Base-to-Flap Ratio. To find a net, identify the base shape. For Pyramids, every edge of that base must have an attached triangle. For Prisms, the rectangles form a continuous strip capped by two identical bases at either end.

Phase 4: The Saboteur

The Trap: The "Face Inventory" decoy. Choosing a net that has 6 triangles for a pentagonal pyramid just because "6 looks like a lot."

The Fix: The Perimeter Match. Count the edges of the base (e.g. Pentagon = 5). That number is exactly how many side-flaps must exist. In a Prism, you need 2 bases; in a Pyramid, you need 1.
