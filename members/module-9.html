Module 9: Sequential Fractions

Phase 1: The X-Ray

In these problems, the "Whole" changes halfway through. You must isolate the Remainder before applying the second fraction.

Advanced Year 2 "Sam has 20 sweets. He eats 4. He gives 1/2 of what is left to Sue. How many does he keep?"
Average Year 4 "A book has 120 pages. I read 1/3 on Monday, then 1/4 of the remainder on Tuesday. How many pages are left?"
Advanced Year 5 "A runner has £400. They spend 2/5 on a hotel and 1/3 of the remainder on travel. Calculate the final balance."

Phase 4: The Saboteur

The "Totalist" Trap: Students often calculate both fractions using the original starting total (e.g. 1/4 of 600 AND 2/5 of 600).

The Fix: Always circle the word "Remainder". It tells you that the first amount has been removed from the box. You must subtract the first result before you even touch the second fraction.
