Module 22: Venn Notation & Logic

Phase 1: The X-Ray

Topic Logic: Modern Set Theory uses symbols to describe relationships. A Cap (∩) is the Intersection (Both), and a Cup (∪) is the Union (Everything in either set). The "Type 2" secret is that the Intersection is counted in BOTH totals, so it must be handled with care.

Advanced Year 2A = {1, 2, 3}, B = {3, 4, 5}. What is the intersection (A ∩ B)?

Advanced Year 5If n(A) = 30, n(B) = 25 and n(A ∪ B) = 45, find n(A ∩ B).

Phase 4: The Saboteur

The "Cup vs Cap" Confusion: Students often mix up ∩ and ∪.

The Fix: Think of the Cap (∩) as a hat that only fits people in the middle. Think of the Cup (∪) as a giant bowl that catches everyone from both sides. Also, remember: Union = Total A + Total B - Intersection. If you don't subtract the cap, you've counted the middle people twice!
