Module 14: Interval Logic

Phase 1: The X-Ray

Interval Logic tackles the "Off-by-One" logic error common in 11+ competitive exams. When calculating the time or distance for a sequence of events, the actual value is contained within the gaps, not the objects. To solve these correctly, you must isolate the number of intervals ($N - 1$) before performing any operations.

Advanced Year 2 "A clock strikes 4 times. If there are 3 seconds between each strike, how much time passes from the first strike to the last?"
Average Year 4 "A 30m straight fence has posts placed every 6m. How many posts are needed to support the entire fence?"
Advanced Year 5 "A patient takes 6 doses of medicine, one every 45 minutes. How many minutes pass between the first and final dose?"

Phase 4: The Saboteur

The "Fence Post" Trap: Dividing total distance by the gap size and assuming that number is the total count of posts. (e.g., $20 \text{m} \div 5 \text{m} = 4 \text{ posts}$). This calculation misses the starting anchor.

The Fix: Use the Anchor Addition Rule. The division ($20 \div 5$) calculates the number of Gaps. To find the number of physical objects, you must add 1 for the "Zero Position" post. Remember: $Gaps + 1 = Objects$.
