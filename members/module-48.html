Module 48: Non-Standard Net Generalization

Phase 1: The X-Ray

Irregular solids follow a predictable "Expansion Rule." To identify any net, you must first count the sides of the **Base Shape**. A Pyramid net attaches one triangle to every edge of that base. A Prism net creates a strip of rectangles and caps both ends with the base shape.

Phase 4: The Saboteur

The Trap: The "Face Counting" decoy. Choosing a net that has the right number of faces but the wrong shapes (e.g., picking 5 squares for a pentagonal pyramid instead of 5 triangles).

The Fix: The Base Match. A prism always has 2 base shapes and N rectangles. A pyramid always has 1 base shape and N triangles. If the base has 8 sides (Octagon), look for exactly 8 side-flaps.
