B1.1: Average Speed — The Big Squash

Phase 1: The X-Ray

To find the average speed, we ignore the "fast" and "slow" bits. We just want to find the One Steady Pace that fits the whole trip.

Level 1: The Road How many miles did we travel altogether? (Add the distances).
Level 2: The Clock How many hours did it take altogether? (Add the times).
Level 3: The Squash Share the total miles into the total hours. That's your speed!

Phase 2: The Anatomy

Point to the Journey Line. We aren't looking at two separate trips anymore; we are watching them merge into one long road.

THE JOURNEY SQUASH
Leg 1: 10 miles
+
Leg 2: 10 miles
→ TOTAL ROAD: 20 MILES

Phase 3: The Engine

→ Total Distance: 30.00 miles
→ Total Time: 3.00 hours
→ Sharing: 30 ÷ 3 = 10
AVERAGE SPEED: 10 mph

Phase 4: The Saboteur

The "Speed Jumble" Trap

If a car goes 10mph then 30mph, children often want to "plus" those numbers to get 40mph. It feels like more numbers should mean a bigger answer!

The Fix: Hide the individual speeds with your hand. Tell your child: "Speeds are invisible until the very end. We only care about the Whole Road and the Whole Clock."
