Module 4: Inverse Proportion

Phase 1: The X-Ray

In Inverse Proportion, the "Total Amount of Work" is a constant brick. If you add more workers, the time squashes down. If you lose workers, the time stretches up.

Adv Year 2 Level "If 2 children take 6 minutes to tidy a room, how long will it take 3 children working just as fast?"
Avg Year 4 Level "4 builders take 10 days to build a wall. How many days would it take 5 builders?"
Adv Year 5 Level (11+) "6 pumps can empty a reservoir in 14 hours. If 2 pumps break down before they start, how long will the remaining pumps take?"

Phase 4: The Saboteur

The "Direct Scaling" Trap: The most common Type 1 error. A student reads "4 builders take 10 days, find 8 builders," and they think: "Double the builders, so double the time (20 days)."

The Fix: Always do the common-sense check. "Will more people take MORE time to do the same job? No."
