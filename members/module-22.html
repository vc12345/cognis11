Triple overlaps require Inside-Out Deduction. Questions usually give you group totals, but you must populate the diagram starting from the innermost core (all three) and work outwards to find unique remainders.

Phase 4: The Saboteur

The "Triple-Counting" Trap: Most students take the group totals and add them together (e.g. 25+25+25 = 75). They forget that people in the "Center" are currently being counted three times!

The Fix: Always anchor the center first. Subtract the center from the overlaps, then subtract those results from the main circles. Work from the center of the "onion" outwards to ensure every person is only counted once.
