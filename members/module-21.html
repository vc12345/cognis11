Module 21: Venn Diagrams (The Intersection)

Phase 1: The X-Ray

Topic Logic: Venn diagrams are visual maps of sets. The most critical reasoning point is the Intersection (∩). When two groups share members, those members are counted in the total of BOTH groups. To find how many are in "Only" one group, we must subtract the intersection.

Level 1In a class, 12 play Football and 3 play Football AND Tennis. How many play only Football?
Level 2Group A has 20 people. Group B has 15. The intersection (A ∩ B) is 5. Find the total Union (A ∪ B).
Level 3There are 40 students. n(A) = 25, n(B) = 20. If 5 students are in neither, how many are in (A ∩ B)?

Phase 4: The Saboteur

The "Double-Counting" Error: If you add the total of A and B (15 + 10 = 25), you have counted the middle 5 people twice.

The Fix: Always think of the Cap (∩) as a shared resource. To find the unique population, you must perform: (A + B) - (A ∩ B). This "flattens" the overlap so every person is counted exactly once.
