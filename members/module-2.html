Module 2: Shared Ratios

Phase 1: The X-Ray

This module identifies questions where a Total Amount must be split using a specific proportion. The key is to see the ratio as a set of equal-sized "boxes" that together hold the entire value.

Advanced Year 2 "Share 15 sweets between Ben and Sam in the ratio 2:1. How many sweets does Ben get?"
Average Year 4 "A £60 prize is split in the ratio 5:7. Calculate the value of the larger share."
Advanced Year 5 "A line 1.5m long is divided in the ratio 3:2. Find the difference in length between the two parts."

Phase 2: The Anatomy

Think of the ratio as a single bar made of equal parts. If you know the Total Value, you must find the value of one part first.

Phase 4: The Saboteur

The Single-Side Trap: Students often divide the total by only one side of the ratio (e.g., £100 ÷ 3).

The Fix: Always SUM the ratio parts first. The Total Value covers the whole bar, so you must divide by the total number of parts to find the value of one share.
