Module 11: Shared Ratios

The "Hidden Whole" Principle

Phase 1: The X-Ray

Questions about "Sharing" or "Ratios" always look like two separate numbers (3:5). But to a Type 2 mind, the numbers aren't 3 and 5. The number is 8 (the total parts).

Phase 2: The Anatomy

Imagine a chocolate bar. If the ratio is 2:3, the bar is split into 5 equal squares. To find the value of one square, you must divide the total by the sum of the ratio.

The Master Plan:
1. Add the ratio together (Total Parts).
2. Total Amount ÷ Total Parts = Value of 1 Part.
3. Multiply by the part you need.
