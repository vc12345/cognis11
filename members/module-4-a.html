The Tidy-Up Test

Usually, in math, "more" means "more." But in Inverse Proportion, more helpers means less time. Think of it as tidying Lego: if you work alone, it takes a long time. If three friends help, the job is finished in a flash!

"Look at the pile of Lego. If we have 1 helper, the clock shows 12 minutes. Now, click to add a friend. Watch how the clock drops! More hands make the work lighter and the time shorter."

The See-Saw Balance

In inverse proportion, there is a Magic Number that never changes. It’s like a See-Saw. If you put more "Workers" on one side, the "Time" on the other side must go down to keep the see-saw perfectly balanced.

"Our 'Magic Number' is the total work. If we have 2 workers taking 6 hours, our Magic Number is 12. If we increase the workers to 4, look at the see-saw—the time must drop to 3 to keep the balance!"

Phase 1: The X-Ray

Inverse proportion is the "Opposite" Rule. If you double the number of workers, you cut the time in half. The secret weapon is finding the Magic Number (Total Work) which never changes. Once you have that total, the answer is just one division away.

Phase 4: The Saboteur

The "More is More" Trap: Bright children often think: "If 4 workers take 10 days, 8 workers must take 20 days." They've doubled the helpers but accidentally made the job take twice as long!

The Fix: The "Lego Test." Ask your child: "If more friends help you tidy Lego, does it take more time or less time?" Once they realize it's less, they will remember to divide the Magic Number by the new helpers.
