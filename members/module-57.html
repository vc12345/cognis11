Module 57: Cloze (Grammatical)

Phase 1: The X-Ray

Grammatical Cloze is the study of Syntactic Slots. Every sentence has a mechanical skeleton: there is always a Noun (the subject) and a Verb (the action). You can expand this skeleton with extra information: Adjectives describe the noun, while Adverbs describe how the verb is being performed. To solve these, you must identify the "Job" the missing word is doing.

Advanced Year 2 "The ______ cat slept."
Slot: Adjective (Noun detail)
Fit: Large / Fluffy
Average Year 4 "He ______ through the park."
Slot: Verb (The Action)
Fit: Sprinting / Walked
Advanced Year 5 "The plane landed ______."
Slot: Adverb (Verb detail)
Fit: Smoothly / Safely

Phase 4: The Saboteur

The "Cool Story" Trap: Choosing a word that makes the story sound interesting, but makes no sense grammatically. For example, picking "Bravely" for the gap in "The _____ explorer" because explorers are brave. Even though it sounds right, "Bravely" is an adverb and cannot describe a noun.

The Fix: Use the Substitution Test. Forget the story and look at the "neighbors." If the word is between "The" and a "Noun," it must be an Adjective. If the word is after an action and ends in -ly, it is an Adverb. Let the grammar dictate the word, not the plot.
