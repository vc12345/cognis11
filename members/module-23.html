Module 23: The Average Family

Phase 1: The X-Ray

Averages identify the "Center" of a group of numbers. The Mean levels the values out fairly, the Median finds the person in the middle of a line-up, and the Mode spots the most popular choice.

Advanced Year 2 "Find the mean of 3, 5, and 7. If you shared these points equally, how many would each person have?"
Average Year 4 "Find the median and range of this data set: 15, 4, 9, 15, 2. Remember to line them up first!"
Advanced Year 5 "The mean age of four children is 12. Three of the children are 10, 11, and 14. How old is the fourth child?"

Phase 2: The Anatomy

Phase 4: The Saboteur

The "Unordered Median" Trap: Attempting to find the Median by simply picking the middle number in the list as it was written (e.g., in the list 8, 2, 5, picking '2' as the middle).

The Fix: The Median is a Physical Rank. You must sort the data from Smallest to Largest before you look for the middle. If there is an even number of items, find the mean of the two central numbers.
