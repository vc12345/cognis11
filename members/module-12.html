Module 12: Relative Speed

Phase 1: The X-Ray

Relative speed describes the rate at which the distance between two objects vanishes. When two objects move toward each other, they are effectively working together to delete the gap. In competitive exams, you must solve these by finding the Closing Rate—the combined sum of both speeds—before calculating the time until impact.

Advanced Year 2 "Two cyclists are 10 miles apart. Both travel at 5mph toward each other. How many hours until they meet?"
Average Year 4 "A runner leaves Point A at 10km/h. A walker leaves Point B (50km away) at 15km/h. When do they pass?"
Advanced Year 5 "Two trains 300 miles apart head toward each other. Train A is 60mph; Train B is 90mph. Where is the impact point?"

Phase 4: The Saboteur

The "Halfway" Trap: Assuming objects moving toward each other always meet exactly in the middle (50% distance). This only happens if their speeds are identical.

The Fix: The impact point is anchored by the Ratio of Speeds. The meeting point will always be closer to the starting point of the slower object. If Speed A is twice as fast as Speed B, Object A will cover 2/3 of the ground.
