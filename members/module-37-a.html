The Stepping Stones

A number series is just a path across a river. To get from one stone to the next, you have to jump the same distance every time. If the first jump is **+2**, then every jump must be **+2** to stay on the path!

"We are standing on stone **2**. To get to the next stone, we jump 2 spaces. Click 'Take Jump' to see the path grow. Notice how we are always adding the same number! This constant jump is the 'Rule' of our sequence."

The Pattern Detective

To solve a mystery series, you have to find the "Hidden Jump" (the interval). Once you find the jump between the first two numbers, you must check if it works for the next pair too. Only then is the rule safe!

Phase 1: The X-Ray

Think of this as "Finding the Rhythm." In the 11+, number series aren't always a steady drumbeat. Sometimes the jump changes (e.g., $+1, +2, +3$) or uses two different operations (e.g., $\times 2, +1$). The secret weapon is finding the Interval of the Interval. If the first jumps don't make sense, look at how the jumps themselves are changing.

Arithmetic "Find the next term: $4, 7, 10, 13...$"
Growing Jumps "Find the next term: $2, 3, 5, 8...$ (The jump is $+1, +2, +3$)."
Alternating "Solve $10, 5, 12, 7...$ (Look for two different sequences in one)."

Phase 2: The Anatomy

The Sequence Engine. Set your starting number and your rule. Watch how the chain grows. Notice that for complex series, the jump is not a single number, but a moving target. Use the "Dual Check" to verify the rhythm.

Phase 3: The Engine

Build a custom pattern. Try a **Growing Jump** (where the interval increases by 1 each time) to see how the numbers accelerate.

Phase 4: The Saboteur

The "One-Jump" Trap: Most children find the jump between the first two numbers ($4$ to $6 = +2$) and immediately use it for the whole line. They don't check if the second jump is different!

The Fix: The "Two-Step Check." "One jump is a guess; two jumps is a rule." Never move your pencil until you have checked the interval between the 1st and 2nd, and the 2nd and 3rd numbers. If they match, you've found the truth.
