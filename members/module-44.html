Module 44: Layering (Z-Order)

Phase 1: The X-Ray

Deciphering depth in a flat image requires identifying which shape's border is continuous. If a shape's perimeter is uninterrupted, it is on top. If its border is "cut" by another shape, it is behind.

Phase 4: The Saboteur

The Trap: The "Shared Edge" Trap. When two shapes share a border of the same color, the brain loses the ability to distinguish depth, often assuming they are a single, merged shape.

The Fix: Corner Logic. Look at the vertices where the shapes meet. If a vertex belongs to Shape A but "points into" Shape B, Shape A is likely on top. Follow the perimeter like a detective—the line that never breaks is the leader of the stack.
