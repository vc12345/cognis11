Pillar 1: Mathematics (Quantitative Reasoning)

Category A: Proportionality & Fractions
The Unitary Method (Basics): Finding the value of one (e.g., 5 cakes cost £10, find 1).
Shared Ratios (Total Known): Dividing a total into parts ($3:2$ ratio of 100).
Shared Ratios (Difference Known): "A has £10 more than B, they are in ratio $5:3$."
Inverse Proportion (Work/Rate): More workers = less time (The $X \times Y = Constant$ rule).
Direct Proportion (Scaling): Recipes and map scales.
Percentage Increase/Decrease: Multiplier logic ($1.10$ for $10\%$ up).
Reverse Percentages: Finding the original price before the sale.
Fractions of Amounts: Basic $X/Y$ of $Z$.
Sequential Fractions: "Fraction of the remainder" (The shrinking pizza).

Category B: Motion & Time
Constant Speed: The $D = S \times T$ triangle.
Average Speed (Harmonic): Two legs of a journey with different speeds.
Relative Speed (Closing the Gap): Two objects moving toward or away from each other.
Train/Tunnel Problems: When the length of the object matters to the distance.
Interval Logic (Clock Chimes/Fence Posts): Counting the gaps, not the objects.
Time Conversions: Crossing the 60-minute boundary (decimal time vs. clock time).

Category C: Operational Logic
The "More Than" Trap: $(Sum - Difference) \div 2$.
Reverse Operations: "Think of a number" (Working backward from the result).
BODMAS/Order of Ops: The hierarchy of mathematical priority.
Missing Signs: Inserting $+/-/ \times / \div$ to make an equation true.
Substitution: Using shapes/letters as placeholders for values.

Category D: Data & Geometry
Venn Diagrams (2 Circles): Handling the "Both" overlap.
Venn Diagrams (3 Circles): The central overlap logic.
Mean/Median/Mode: The "Average" family.
The Mean Bridge: "If the mean of 4 numbers is $X$, and a 5th is added..."
Combinatorics (Handshakes): $n(n-1) \div 2$.
Area/Perimeter Invariance: Different shapes with the same boundary.
Angle Logic: Rules of parallel lines, triangles, and circles.
Net-to-Volume: Visualizing capacity from a 2D layout.

Pillar 2: Verbal Reasoning (Linguistic & Symbolic Logic)

Category E: Coding & Decoding
Alpha-Numeric Shift: $A=1, B=2$ logic.
Relative Letter Shift: $CAT \rightarrow ECV$ (Fixed offset).
Vowel/Consonant Ciphers: Rules that treat vowels differently.
Symbol-to-Word Mapping: Breaking a code where $\Delta = G$.

Category F: Word Mechanics
Hidden Words: Spanning the seam between two words.
Compound Word Bridge: One word that completes two others.
Word Morphing (Moving Letters): Moving one letter from Word A to B to make two new words.
Anagram Pairs: Identifying sets of words made of the same letters.
Missing Letters: Completing a word with a single letter that fits multiple gaps.

Category G: Semantic Logic
Synonym/Antonym Precision: Choosing the "best" fit from near-misses.
Relationship Analogies: "Part-to-Whole" vs "Function-to-Object."
Odd One Out (Semantic): Identifying the rule that excludes one word.
Logical Ordering: Ranking by height, weight, or time based on clues.

Pillar 3: Non-Verbal Reasoning (Spatial Reasoning)

Category H: 2D Transformations
Rotational Invariance: Identifying a shape regardless of its spin.
Reflection Logic: Mapping across horizontal, vertical, and diagonal axes.
Layering (Z-Order): Determining which shape is on top.
Attribute Matrices: 3x3 grids where Color, Shape, and Size move independently.
Series Progression: Tracking a "walking" element through a sequence.

Category I: 3D & Manipulation
Net Folding (Standard Cube): The "Skip-One" adjacency rule.
Net Folding (Non-Standard): Folding irregular 3D shapes.

Rotated Cubes: Identifying if a 3D cube is the same as another.
Paper Folding (The Hole Punch): Predicting symmetry after unfolding.
Block Counting: Identifying hidden blocks in a 3D stack.

Pillar 4: English (Textual & Grammatical Logic)

Category J: Comprehension Logic
Explicit Retrieval: "The answer is in the text."
Implicit Inference: "The answer is suggested by the text."
Tone & Purpose: Identifying the author's "Why."
Fact vs. Opinion: Distinguishing objective data from subjective voice.

Category K: Applied Linguistics
Cloze (Contextual): Filling gaps based on story meaning.
Cloze (Grammatical): Filling gaps based on Word Class (Noun/Verb/Adj).
Punctuation Mechanics: Semicolons, colons, and the "Comma Splice."
Tense Consistency: Identifying when a passage "slips" between past and present.0
