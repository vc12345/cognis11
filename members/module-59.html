Module 59: Tense Consistency

Phase 1: The X-Ray

Tense is the Logic of Time. We need different tenses to time-stamp actions: Simple Past for finished acts, Present for habits, and Future for intent. Most errors occur when a writer "slips" between timelines mid-sentence. However, you can use two tenses together if one action interrupts another (e.g., "I was walking [Past Continuous] when it started [Simple Past] to rain").

Advanced Year 2 "She played tag and then eats a snack."
Error: Slip from Past to Present.
Average Year 4 "They arrived late, so they miss the start."
Error: Timeline mismatch.
Advanced Year 5 "While the team was winning, the rain stops the match."
Error: Broken Interruption logic.

Phase 2: The Anatomy

The Temporal Interruption. When using two tenses together, they must follow a specific hierarchy. The "Long Action" provides the background, while the "Short Action" breaks the timeline.

Background: "I was eating"
Trigger: "Phone Rang"
Result: "I stopped"

Phase 4: The Saboteur

The "Dialogue" Trap: Attempting to "fix" the tense inside a character's speech. For example, in the sentence: The boy said, "I am happy." The "said" is past, but the "am" is present. This is 100% correct because talking happens in its own time.

The Fix: Use the Anchor Verb Test. Identify the very first verb in the *narrative* (not the dialogue). This is your Anchor. Every other descriptive action must match the Anchor's time-stamp. If the Anchor is past, every other description must bow to the past.
