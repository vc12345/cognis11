Module 3: Shared Ratios (The Gap)

Foundation Intro

The Chunk Finder

Solving a ratio is all about finding the "Secret Value" hidden inside just one chunk. Think of a ratio as a chocolate bar. If we know how much one square is worth, we know everything! We find this by squashing the total money into every single square of the bar.

"We have £40 to share. The ratio is 3 : 1. That means we have 4 chunks total. If we squash the £40 into all 4 chunks, how much is in just one square? That's our master key!"

The "Box Rule"

Tell your child: "Focus on the gold chunk." The whole point of the math is to find the value of exactly ONE box. Once you have that, the rest of the problem is just simple multiplication.

Foundation Progressing

The Gravity of the Gap

Sometimes, we don't know the "Total." We only know the Gap (the difference). In a ratio, the "Gap Money" only lives in the Extra Parts. Whichever side has more parts is heavier, and that extra weight is where the difference hides.

"Side A has 5 parts and Side B has 2 parts. Look at the scale—Side A is much heavier! The 3 extra parts are what 'pull' the extra £30 into Side A's pocket. We only divide the gap money by those extra parts!"

The "Tie-Breaker" Secret

If the parts are equal, there is no gap! The "Gap Money" always belongs to the Subtraction Result (the difference between the two ratio numbers). Always subtract the ratio parts first when you hear the words "more than."

Phase 1: The X-Ray

In the 11+, the examiner often hides the "Total" and only tells you the Gap (e.g., "Person A has £10 more than B"). The secret weapon is realizing that this money only lives in the Extra Boxes. If your child can see that the "Extra £10" belongs to the "2 Extra Boxes," they can find the value of one box and solve the whole puzzle instantly.

The Pocket Money "Money shared 3:1. Person A has £10 more than B. How much does B have?"
The Classroom "The ratio of boys to girls is 5:2. There are 15 more boys than girls. Find the girls."
The Baker's Mix "Flour and sugar are in ratio 7:3. There is 200g more flour than sugar. Find the total mass."

Phase 4: The Saboteur

The "Total Blindness" Trap: Most children see a number and immediately add the ratio parts together to divide. This is a disaster if the number is actually a Gap.

The Fix: The "Minus First" Rule. Remind your child: "If you see the words 'more than' or 'difference,' you must subtract the ratio numbers first." (5 - 3 = 2). The "Gap Money" only fits into the "Gap Boxes."
