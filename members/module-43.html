Module 43: Reflection Logic

Phase 1: The X-Ray

Reflection is a "Mirror Operation." In a 2D space, this operation reverses the "handedness" of a shape. To solve these, don't just move the shape—you must flip it so that every point is the exact same distance from the axis as its original counterpart.

Average Year 4Perform a vertical reflection on an "L" shape.

Phase 4: The Saboteur

The Trap: The "Slide" Illusion. When performing a sequence (like reflecting twice across the same axis), students often think the shape has rotated.

The Fix: The "Asymmetry Check." Look at the "arms" of the F. If the arms pointed right and now they point left, you have performed a reflection. If you reflect it *again* across the same axis, it must return exactly to its original state. Reflections always come in pairs—two flips across the same line = original state.
