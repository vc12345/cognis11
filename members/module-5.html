Module 5: Percentage Change

THE "OPERATOR" PRINCIPLE

Phase 1: The X-Ray

A "Type 1" thinker treats a percentage like a standard number (e.g., £80 + 10% = £90). A "Type 2" thinker knows that 10% is not a value—it is an operation performed on the original amount.

Exam Wording Patterns:
1. "A bike normally costs £120 but is in a sale with 20% off."
2. "Add 20% VAT to a plumbing bill of £300."
3. "A house increases in value by 5% from its original £200,000 price."

Phase 2: The Anatomy

Think of the original amount as 100%. To find any other percentage, you should find a "Building Block" like 10% or 1% first.

Step 1: Find the value of the percentage (Change).
Step 2: Add it (Increase) or Subtract it (Decrease) from the 100% original.
