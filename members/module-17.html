Phase 1: The X-Ray

In "Reverse Operations," we work from the Result back to the Start. Every action has an opposite equal reaction.

  • Entry (Y2): I think of a number. I add 7. The result is 15. What was my number?
  • Standard (Y4): I think of a number. I multiply it by 4, then add 10. The result is 50. What was my number?
  • Advanced (Y5): I think of a number. I divide by 2, subtract 5, then multiply by 3. The result is 15. What was my number?

Phase 4: The Saboteur

The "Forward Chronology" Trap: Students often try to apply operations in the order they were written. If the problem says "multiply then add," they try to multiply the result!

The Fix: The last thing that happened is the first thing you undo. If you put your socks on then your shoes, you must take your shoes off before you can take your socks off. Last in, First out.
