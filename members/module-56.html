Module 56: Cloze (Contextual)

Phase 1: The X-Ray

Contextual Cloze logic requires you to find the Semantic Fit. Every sentence has "Anchor Words" that act like magnets, pulling for a specific meaning. If a sentence mentions "heavy clouds" and "thunder," the missing word is likely "rain," not "sunshine."

Advanced Year 2The _____ cat purred as it curled up by the warm fire.

Clue: Purred/Warm fire
Fit: Content / Sleepy
Average Year 4After the long hike, the scouts were so _____ that they ate their dinner in seconds.

Clue: Long hike/Ate in seconds
Fit: Famished / Starving
Advanced Year 5The politician’s _____ speech failed to convince the skeptical crowd.

Clue: Failed to convince/Skeptical
Fit: Vague / Unconvincing

Phase 2: The Anatomy

Parent Instruction: Tell your child to "Read past the gap." Often, the word that tells you the answer comes after the blank space. If they stop at the blank, they miss the biggest clue.

Phase 3: The Engine

Phase 4: The Saboteur

The Trap

The "Sounds Right" Trap. Students often pick a word simply because it sounds like a common phrase, ignoring the rest of the sentence. If they see "The hiker felt...", they might pick "happy" because it's a common sentence, even if the text mentions a disaster.

The Fix

The Evidence Check. Before finalizing a word, you must find at least two other words in the sentence that support it. If you pick "Parched," your evidence is "Grueling climb" and "Empty bottle." If you can't find evidence, the word is a trap.
