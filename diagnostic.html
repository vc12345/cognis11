Is your child a
Deep Understander?

We'll compare your observations against two distinct cognitive profiles to identify the preparation path your child actually needs.

Observation Checklist:

Profile Type A

The Deep Understander

✓
Asks "why" more than "what" — wants the underlying logic.
"But why does multiplying by a fraction make it smaller?"
✓
Pauses to understand before acting — avoids rushing.
Waits until all rules are explained before starting a game.
✓
Performance drops noticeably under time pressure.
Gets most things right with no clock; makes careless errors in timed tests.
✓
Performs well on unfamiliar or unusual problems.
Tricky word problems often get a better response than simple drills.
✓
Explains reasoning clearly and step-by-step.
"I knew it was subtraction because the total was already there."
✓
Bored or plateauing on repetitive drilling.
Visibly loses focus after 8 or 9 of the same question type.
✓
Persists until a specific gap is understood.
Will dwell on one single word they don't know before moving on.
Profile Type B

The Pattern Recogniser

✓
Jumps into problems quickly — trusts first instincts.
Starts writing an answer while the problem is still being read.
✓
Spots patterns confidently but struggles to explain "how".
"I just knew the next number was 32."
✓
Speed feels natural; finishes timed tests with ease.
Timed papers don't seem to affect their accuracy much.
✓
Scores improve steadily with high-volume practice.
Confidence grows the more they see the same format.
✓
Accepts a correction and moves on without dwelling.
"Oh, it's 12. Okay, next one."
✓
Restless when asked to plan or reflect before doing.
Wants to start building/doing immediately rather than analyzing.
✓
Struggles with novel questions phrased in unfamiliar ways.
A simple maths problem hidden in a weird story throws them off.
