Module 13: Train & Tunnel Problems

Phase 1: The X-Ray

A train passing a point (like a pole) only travels its own length. But to "clear" a tunnel, the train must travel the tunnel's length PLUS its own length.

Entry"A train 100m long passes a signal post at 20m/s. How long does it take?"
Standard"A train 200m long travels through a 400m tunnel at 72km/h. How long until it is completely clear?"
Advanced"A train takes 15s to pass a pole and 45s to pass a 600m tunnel. Find the train's speed."

Phase 4: The Saboteur

The "Front-Only" Trap: Students often calculate how long it takes for the front of the train to reach the end of the tunnel.

The Fix: The train isn't "clear" until its tail leaves. You must visualize the train's nose traveling the tunnel length AND then traveling its own length to get the tail out.
