Module 3: Reverse Mean

THE "HIDDEN TOTAL" PRINCIPLE

Phase 1: The X-Ray

A "Type 1" thinker looks at averages and tries to find the middle. A "Type 2" thinker knows that behind every average is a Total Sum. To find a missing value, you must find the new Total and subtract the old Total.

Exam Wording Patterns:
1. "The mean score of 4 tests is 70%. What must the 5th score be for an average of 75%?"
2. "5 people have an average age of 20. When a 6th joins, the average is 21. How old is the 6th?"
3. "The average weight of 3 boxes is 5kg. Adding a 4th box makes the average 6kg."

Phase 2: The Anatomy

You cannot compare means directly. You must convert them into Totals.

Step 1: Old Mean × Old Count = Old Total.
Step 2: New Mean × New Count = New Total.
Step 3: New Total - Old Total = The Missing Value.
