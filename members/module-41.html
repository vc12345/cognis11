Module 41: Logical Ordering

Phase 1: The X-Ray

Logical ordering is the art of possibility elimination. Instead of trying to guess the one correct order, we acknowledge every possible permutation and use linguistic clues as "filters." By removing what is inconsistent, the only remaining option must be the truth.

A is taller than B.

Phase 4: The Saboteur

The Trap: Fixing an item in a specific position (e.g., "Ben is 1st") based on an early clue, only to find a later clue contradicts it. This creates a "mental knot" that is hard to undo.

The Fix: Permutation Filtering. Don't commit to a position until the logic forces it. Treat clues as "Rules of Exclusion." If the rule says "A > B," then every arrangement where B comes before A is instantly deleted.
