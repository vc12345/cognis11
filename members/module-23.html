Module 23: Coordinate Geometry

THE "CORRIDOR & STAIRS" PRINCIPLE

Phase 1: The X-Ray

A "Type 1" thinker mixes up X and Y. A "Type 2" thinker remembers: X is across (like a cross), and Y is high (the tail goes down, but it points up).

1. "Plot the point (-3, 4)."
2. "What are the coordinates of point A?"
3. "Translate the point 2 units right and 3 units down."

Phase 2: The Anatomy

( X , Y )
X: Left (-) or Right (+)
Y: Down (-) or Up (+)

Phase 3: The Engine

(0, 0)

Phase 4: The Saboteur

The "Reversal" Trap: Swapping X and Y (e.g., going up 3 then right 2 for (3,2)). This is the #1 cause of lost marks in spatial reasoning.
