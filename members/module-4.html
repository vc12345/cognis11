Module 4: Inverse Proportion

Phase 1: The X-Ray

In inverse proportion, the "Total Amount of Work" is a Fixed Constant. Unlike direct proportion, where variables move together, these variables are linked by a product. If you double the number of workers, the time must be cut in half. To solve these, you must find the constant product first:

$$\text{Workers} \times \text{Time} = \text{Total Work (Constant)}$$
Advanced Year 2 "If 2 children take 6 minutes to tidy a room, how long will it take 3 children working at the same speed?"
Average Year 4 "4 builders take 10 days to build a wall. How many days would it take 5 builders to complete the same task?"
Advanced Year 5 "6 pumps empty a tank in 14 hours. If 2 pumps break down before starting, how long will the remaining pumps take?"

Phase 4: The Saboteur

The "Direct Scaling" Trap: Mistakenly applying direct logic to an inverse problem. (e.g., If 4 workers take 10 days, 8 workers must take 20 days.) You are doubling the speed, but doubling the time as well.

The Fix: Use the Common Sense Filter. Always ask: "If I have more help, should the job take more or less time?" If the answer is "less," you must divide the constant by the new worker count, not multiply.
