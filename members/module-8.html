Module 8: Fractions of Amounts

Phase 1: The X-Ray

A fraction is not a static number; it is a Mechanical Instruction. The Denominator (bottom) tells you how many equal groups to cut the total into. The Numerator (top) tells you how many of those groups to collect. To solve any "Fraction of an Amount" problem, always find the value of exactly one part first.

Advanced Year 2 "Find 1/2 of 12 sweets. How many are in one group?"
Average Year 4 "A cake weighs 500g and is cut into 5 slices. If 3 slices are eaten, what is the weight consumed?"
Advanced Year 5 "A 40km race is 5/8 complete. How many kilometers are left to run? (Hint: Find 3/8)."

Phase 2: The Anatomy

The Partition Bar. Visualizing the denominator as the physical "cutter" that creates equal units. Notice how each unit carries an identical portion of the total value.

Phase 4: The Saboteur

The "Upside Down" Trap: Getting confused about which number performs which job. Students often try to divide the total by the numerator (the top) because it is the first number they see.

The Fix: Use the Cutter-Collector Rule. The number on the Bottom is the "Cutter"—it divides the total into equal groups. The number on the Top is the "Collector"—it gathers those groups. Always "Divide by the Bottom, Multiply by the Top."
