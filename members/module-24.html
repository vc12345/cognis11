Module 24: The Mean Bridge

Phase 1: The X-Ray

A Mean is a "Fair Share" height. To find a missing member of a set, you must compare the Total Height of the current group against the Total Height required to hit the target average.

Advanced Year 2 "The mean of 3 numbers is 5. Two of the numbers are 4 and 6. What is the 3rd number?"
Average Year 4 "Sarah scored 12, 15, and 10 in her tests. What must she score in her 4th test to have a mean of 13?"
Advanced Year 5 "The mean weight of 5 children is 40kg. When a 6th child joins, the mean becomes 41kg. Find the weight of the new child."

Phase 2: The Anatomy

The "Bridge" is the difference between the totals. Dropping the Missing Data into the left group levels it up to match the target.

Phase 4: The Saboteur

The "Average of Averages" Trap: Trying to find the missing score by averaging the current mean and the target mean. If your current mean is 10 and you want to reach 13, it is tempting to think the next score is 16. This logic breaks as soon as the group size changes.

The Fix: Mean problems are Total Volume problems in disguise. Always find the new total (New Count × New Mean) and the old total (Old Count × Old Mean). The difference is always your answer.
