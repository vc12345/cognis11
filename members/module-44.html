Module 44: Layering (Z-Order)

Phase 1: The X-Ray

Layering logic requires you to translate a flat 2D image into 3D depth. The core rule is Perimeter Continuity. If a shape's border is unbroken, it is on top. If a shape's border is "interrupted" or cut by another line, it is physically behind. The brain uses these occlusion cues to calculate the sequence of the stack.

Advanced Year 2 "A red circle overlaps a blue square. If you can see the full circle but the square is cut, which is on top?"
Average Year 4 "Look at three overlapping shapes. List them in order from the one at the back to the one at the very front."
Advanced Year 5 "Identify the 'Hidden Center' shape. This is a shape that is partially covered by two others, leaving only a fragment visible."

Phase 4: The Saboteur

The "Shared Border" Trap: When two shapes have the same color or a very thin border, your brain might merge them into a single complex shape. This causes you to miss the fact that one is actually behind the other.

The Fix: Use the Vertex Hunt. Ignore the large areas of color and focus entirely on the "T-junctions" (where lines meet). If Shape A's line stops at Shape B's edge, Shape A is at the back. The "winner" is the shape whose line never stops for anyone.
