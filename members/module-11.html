Module 11: Average Speed

Phase 1: The X-Ray

Average speed is not the average of the speeds. It is the total story of the journey. You must collapse all segments into one single distance and one single time.

Adv Year 2"A car travels 10 miles in the morning and 20 miles in the afternoon. The whole trip took 3 hours. What was the average speed?"
Avg Year 4"A cyclist travels 15km at 15km/h, then 20km at 10km/h. What is the average speed for the whole journey?"
Adv Year 5"A plane flies 400 miles at 200mph, then 600 miles at 300mph. Calculate the average speed for the 1000-mile flight."

Phase 4: The Saboteur

The "Mean Speed" Trap: If a car goes 40mph then 60mph, students often say the average is 50mph ($ (40+60) \div 2 $).

The Fix: This only works if the *times* spent at each speed are identical. Since journeys usually vary, you must always find Total Distance and Total Time first.
