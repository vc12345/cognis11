Module 34: Compound Word Bridge

Phase 1: The X-Ray

Compound word bridges are Hinge Words—linguistic connectors that serve two masters simultaneously. To solve these, you must identify a single word that acts as the suffix for the first word and the prefix for the second. This requires dual-process thinking: maintaining one successful connection while scanning for a second.

Advanced Year 2 "Find the word that fits in the middle: RAIN [ ____ ] BALL. It must make a word with both."
Average Year 4 "Find the 4-letter bridge: FOOT [ ____ ] ROOM. Both words must be common compound nouns."
Advanced Year 5 "Identify the bridge for SEA [ ____ ] SIDE. The connection must work perfectly at both junctions."

Phase 4: The Saboteur

The "Half-Match" Trap: Finding a word that connects perfectly to the first block and assuming it's correct without checking the second (e.g., choosing COAT for RAIN-COAT, even though COAT-BALL is not a word).

The Fix: Use the Tug-of-War Test. Once you find a candidate for the first word, mentally "pull" it over to the second. If it doesn't click into place to form a common compound word, discard it immediately. A bridge only counts if it supports both sides.
