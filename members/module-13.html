Module 13: Train & Tunnel Problems

Phase 1: The X-Ray

A train passing a point (like a pole) only travels its own length. But a train passing through a tunnel must travel its own length plus the tunnel's length to be fully "clear."

Entry"A train 100m long passes a signal post at 20m/s. How long does it take?"
Standard"A train 200m long travels through a 400m tunnel at 60km/h. How long until it is completely clear?"
Advanced"A train takes 15 seconds to pass a telegraph pole and 45 seconds to pass through a tunnel 600m long. How long is the train?"

Phase 4: The Saboteur

The "Half-Exit" Trap: Students often calculate the time for the front of the train to exit.

The Fix: The problem usually asks for the time to "pass through" or "clear" the tunnel. This only happens when the tail of the train exits. Always add the lengths!
