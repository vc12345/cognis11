Module 6: Percentage Increase & Decrease

Phase 1: The X-Ray

Mastering percentages means moving beyond "finding 10% and adding it." In the 11+ exam, speed is vital. We use Multipliers to jump straight to the final answer. By treating the original value as 100% (1.00), an increase becomes $1.00 + \%$, and a decrease becomes $1.00 - \%$. This turns complex problems into a single multiplication step.

Advanced Year 2 "A £20 teddy is 10% off in a sale. How much money is saved?"
Average Year 4 "A bag of 40 marbles gets a '20% extra free' bonus. How many are in the bag now?"
Advanced Year 5 "A store increases prices by 15%. If a game cost £40, what is the new price? Solve using one multiplier."

Phase 2: The Anatomy

The Multiplier Shortcut. Notice how the multiplier is anchored at 1.00. Adding to it causes growth; subtracting from it causes shrinkage. The resulting decimal is the single key that unlocks the final value.

Phase 4: The Saboteur

The "Literal Addition" Trap: If a price is £50 and increases by 10%, many students simply add the number 10 (e.g. £50 + 10 = £60). You are adding a fixed integer instead of a proportion.

The Fix: Percentages are always fractions of a specific base. Always convert the percentage into a decimal multiplier ($1.1$ for a 10% increase) before touching the original number. This ensures the "extra" amount is proportional to the starting price.
