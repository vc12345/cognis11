Module 22: Venn Diagrams (3 Circles)

Phase 1: The X-Ray

Topic Logic: 3-Circle Venns introduce **Triple Intersections**. Information is often presented as total group sizes, but to build the diagram, you must work from the innermost core (all three) outward. This prevents the "Accumulation Error" where students count the same person 3 or 4 times.

EntryIn a group of 20, 5 like A, B, and C. 8 like A and B. How many like A and B, but NOT C?
StandardA survey of 50 people: 25 drink Tea, 20 Coffee, 15 Juice. 10 drink Tea and Coffee. 5 drink all three. 2 drink none. Find 'Only Tea'.
Advanced$n(A)=40, n(B)=35, n(C)=30$. $n(A\cap B)=15, n(B\cap C)=12, n(A\cap C)=10$. If 5 like all three, find the total population.

Phase 4: The Saboteur

The "Totalist" Trap: If the question says "20 people like Apples" and "5 people like everything," the number in the Apple-Only section is **not** 20.

The Fix: Every circle in a Venn diagram is a container. The "Everything" people are already inside the Apple container. You must **subtract** them to find who likes *only* Apples.
