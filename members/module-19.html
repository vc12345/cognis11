Phase 4: The Saboteur

The "Linear" Trap: Most students read equations left-to-right like a story. For example, if they see 2 + 3 to the power of 2, they might say 2 + 3 = 5, then 5 squared = 25.

The Fix: Use the Priority Filter. Roots and Powers are "Heavy" operators. They must be solved before addition or subtraction can even be considered. In the example above, 3 squared is solved first (9), making the true weight 11.
