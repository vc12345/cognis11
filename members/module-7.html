Module 7: Reverse Percentages

Phase 1: The X-Ray

Reverse Percentages ask you to calculate the Original Starting Point. You are given the "Destination" (the final price) and the "Journey" (the percentage change). To find the start, you must drive backwards. Success depends on recognizing that a sale price is the result of a multiplier; to undo it, you must use Division.

Advanced Year 2 "A half-price toy costs £5. What was the full price before the sale?"
Average Year 4 "After a 10% discount, a pair of trainers costs £45. Find the original price."
Advanced Year 5 "A house is now worth £240,000 after a 20% increase in value. What was its previous value?"

Phase 2: The Anatomy

The Reverse Bridge. Normal percentages move left-to-right via multiplication. Reverse percentages move right-to-left by applying the Inverse Operation: Division by the Multiplier.

Original £200
× 1.20
÷ 1.20
Final £240

Phase 4: The Saboteur

The "Forward Percent" Trap: If a price is £90 after a 10% discount, many students find 10% of £90 (£9) and add it back to get £99. This is wrong because the 10% was taken off the original larger number, not the sale price.

The Fix: Never calculate a percentage of the sale price. Always identify the Decimal Multiplier first (e.g. 0.90 for a 10% discount) and then DIVIDE the sale price by that multiplier. Division naturally "stretches" the number back to its original size.
