Module 32: Symbol-to-Word Mapping

Phase 1: The X-Ray

Cryptanalysis is the science of breaking codes by analyzing Topology. If a symbol repeats, the letter it represents must also repeat in the same position. Look for word structures—like the ABA pattern in the word "MOM"—to identify specific letters and cross-reference them across the set.

Phase 4: The Saboteur

The "Visual Mimic" Trap: Trying to "guess" letters based on what a symbol looks like (e.g., assuming a Circle must be the letter 'O' or a Bolt must be 'S').

The Fix: Ignore the shape; follow the Position. If a symbol appears at the start and end of a 3-letter word, you are looking for an ABA structure (like DID, POP, or EVE). Logic beats visual guessing every time.
