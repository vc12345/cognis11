Module 52: Explicit Retrieval

Phase 1: The X-Ray

Explicit Retrieval is the "Treasure Hunt" of comprehension. You are not looking for meaning; you are looking for Surface Data. By identifying unique Anchor Words in the question (numbers, capitals, or dates), you can scan the text and "retrieve" the answer without needing to interpret the story.

Q: What pattern was the rug?
Average Year 4The patent for the steam engine was filed in 1784 by James Watt.
Q: In which year was the patent filed?
Advanced Year 5The expedition arrived at the peak at 14:22 GMT despite the blizzard.
Q: At what precise time did they arrive?

Phase 4: The Saboteur

The Trap: The Synonym Shift. The question asks for the "location," but the text says "destination." If you scan only for the exact word in the question, your brain will "miss" the data even when looking right at it.

The Fix: The Category Pivot. Don't scan for words; scan for Data Classes. If the question asks "Where," your eyes should only be looking for capitalized Proper Nouns. If it asks "When," look only for digits. By ignoring vocabulary and focusing on structure, you bypass the trap.
