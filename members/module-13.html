Module 13: Ratio Sharing

THE "PARTS" PRINCIPLE

Phase 1: The X-Ray

A "Type 1" thinker divides the total by one side of the ratio. A "Type 2" thinker knows that the ratio represents "Parts"—so you must add the parts together before you divide.

Exam Wording Patterns:
1. "Share £60 between Ben and Jerry in the ratio 2:3."
2. "A 50cm rope is cut in a ratio of 1:4. How long is the shortest piece?"
3. "Ingredients are mixed in a ratio of 3 parts flour to 1 part sugar. Total weight is 400g."

Phase 2: The Anatomy

Step 1: Add the parts (e.g., 2 + 3 = 5 total parts).
Step 2: Divide (Total ÷ Total Parts) to find the value of 1 part.
Step 3: Multiply (1 Part × Ratio Side) for each share.
