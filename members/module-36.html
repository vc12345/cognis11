Phase 1: The X-Ray

An anagram is a word or phrase formed by rearranging the letters of another. To a computer (and a Type 2 thinker), two words are anagrams if their Sorted Inventory matches exactly. The order is irrelevant; the stock is everything.

Phase 4: The Saboteur

The "One Letter Off" Trap: Words like DESSERT and DESERT are designed to fool the eye. They share the same letters, but not the same count.

The Fix: Never trust your eyes. Trust the inventory. If Word A has two 'S's and Word B has one, the "Sorted Key" will break, proving they are not anagrams.
