Module 12: Reverse Percentages

THE "BACK TO 100" PRINCIPLE

Phase 1: The X-Ray

This is the most dangerous trap in the exam. A "Type 1" thinker sees a 10% increase and tries to "fix" it by taking 10% off the new price. A "Type 2" thinker knows the original 100% is gone and must be found again.

Exam Wording Patterns:
1. "After a 20% increase, a TV costs £240. What was the original price?"
2. "A bag of sweets has 10% extra free. It now has 66 sweets. How many originally?"
3. "A sale price is £45 after a 10% reduction. What was the price before?"

Phase 2: The Anatomy

The key is realizing that the Final Value is not 100%. If there was a 20% increase, the Final Value is 120%.

The Method:
1. Final Value ÷ (100 + Increase) = Value of 1%
2. Value of 1% × 100 = Original Price (100%)

Step 1: £ represents %.

Step 2 (Find 1%): £ ÷ = £

Step 3 (Find 100%): £ × 100 = £
