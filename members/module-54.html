Module 54: Tone & Purpose

Phase 1: The X-Ray

In reading comprehension, you must distinguish between Voice and Goal. Tone is the emotional temperature of the text (how the author feels), while Purpose is the mechanical intent (why the author wrote it). By auditing the adjectives and verbs separately, you can identify if a text is designed to inform, persuade, or ridicule.

Advanced Year 2 "Eat your greens now, or no pudding!"
Tone: Stern / Authoritative
Purpose: To Instruct
Average Year 4 "The sun’s golden rays kissed the calm, blue ocean."
Tone: Peaceful / Serene
Purpose: To Describe
Advanced Year 5 "The 'new' law is as effective as a paper umbrella in a storm."
Tone: Sarcastic / Cynical
Purpose: To Ridicule

Phase 4: The Saboteur

The "Subject" Trap: Mistaking the *topic* for the *tone*. For example, assuming a story about a rainy day must be "sad." You are reacting to the event, not the author's voice.

The Fix: Use the Adjective Audit. Strip the story away and look only at the descriptive words. If a sad event is described using "recorded," "standard," and "precise," the tone is Clinical, not sad. The tone lives in the word choice, not the plot.
