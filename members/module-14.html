This is the classic "Off-by-One" error. In math problems involving rows, chimes, or laps, the Distance or Time happens in the spaces between the objects.

Standard"A fence 20m long has posts every 5m. How many posts are used?"
Advanced"A doctor tells a patient to take a pill every 30 mins. The patient has 5 pills. How long until they have finished all of them?"

Phase 4: The Saboteur

The "Total Division" Trap: If a clock strikes 6 times in 30 seconds, students calculate $30 \div 6 = 5$ seconds per interval.

The Fix: There are only 5 intervals between 6 strikes. The correct math is $30 \div 5 = 6$ seconds. Always subtract 1 from the number of objects to find the number of "intervals" or "gaps."
