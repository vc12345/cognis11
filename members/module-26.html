Module 26: Area/Perimeter Invariance

Phase 1: The X-Ray

Perimeter is 1-dimensional length; Area is 2-dimensional space. While a boundary may remain a fixed length, the internal capacity fluctuates based on the shape's balance. A square configuration maximizes the internal area for any given perimeter.

Phase 4: The Saboteur

For a fixed perimeter, the closer a rectangle is to a square, the greater its area. When the sides are extremely unequal (long and thin), the area collapses toward zero, even though the total boundary length remains unchanged.
