Module 8: Fractions of Amounts

Phase 1: The X-Ray

A fraction is an instruction: the Denominator (bottom) tells you how many equal groups to make. The Numerator (top) tells you how many of those groups to take.

Adv Year 2"Find 1/2 of 12 sweets."
Avg Year 4"A cake is cut into 5 equal pieces. If 3 pieces are eaten, and the whole cake weighed 500g, how much was eaten?"
Adv Year 5"A marathon is 40km. A runner has completed 5/8 of the race. How many kilometers are left to run?"

Phase 4: The Saboteur

The "Upside Down" Trap: Students often get confused about which number to divide by. They might try to divide the total by the numerator.

The Fix: Remember the phrase: "Divide by the Bottom, Multiply by the Top." The denominator is the 'cutter' (it divides the shape), and the numerator is the 'collector'.
