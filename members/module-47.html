Module 47: Standard Net Folding

Phase 1: The X-Ray

A cube net is a flat "skin" that folds into a 3D object. The most powerful shortcut is identifying Opposite Faces. If two faces are opposite, they can never share an edge on the final cube. If you see them touching in an answer choice, that choice is a lie.

Advanced Year 2

Identify which side is opposite the "Top" in a simple cross net.

Average Year 4

Use the "Skip-One" rule to find three pairs of opposites in a "T" net.

Advanced Year 5

Solve a net where all faces have complex, directional symbols (like arrows).

Phase 4: The Saboteur

The Trap: The "Touching Corner" Illusion. Thinking two faces are opposites just because they look far apart on the flat net.

The Fix: The L-Rule. If you move two squares in one direction and one square to the side, those two squares are almost always opposites. But the "Skip-One" is the gold standard: in a straight line, skip one square to find the enemy face.
