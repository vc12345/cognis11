Module 25: Combinatorics (Handshakes)

Phase 1: The X-Ray

Combinatorics is the science of counting unique possibilities. In "Interaction" problems (handshakes, high-fives, or league matches), we must calculate how many ways a pair can be formed from a group. Success requires the Logic of Elimination: removing "Self-Interactions" and "Reflections" from the total grid.

Advanced Year 2 "4 friends high-five each other once. How many high-fives happen total?"
Average Year 4 "8 teams play in a tournament. If every team plays every other team once, how many games are scheduled?"
Advanced Year 5 "A total of 45 handshakes occurred in a room. How many people were present?"

Phase 2: The Anatomy

The Matrix of Possibility. A group of $N$ people creates an $N \times N$ grid. Notice how we must delete the diagonal line (people cannot shake their own hands) and exactly half of the remaining grid (to remove duplicate pairs).

Phase 4: The Saboteur

The "Reflection" Trap: If there are 10 people, calculating $10 \times 9 = 90$ counts every interaction twice. It counts Person A shaking Person B, and then Person B shaking Person A as separate events.

The Fix: Look at the visual matrix. The top-right triangle is a perfect mirror of the bottom-left. In combinatorics, we only care about Unique Pairings. Therefore, you must always divide by 2 to discard the reflections.
