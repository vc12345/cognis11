Module 43: Reflection Logic

Phase 1: The X-Ray

Reflection is a **Mirror Operation** that reverses spatial "handedness." Unlike rotation, which maintains the internal sequence of parts, reflection swaps them across the axis. To solve these, track the distance of the furthest point from the mirror—it must remain the furthest point in the reflected result.

Phase 4: The Saboteur

The Trap: The "Slide" Illusion. Thinking a reflection just moves a shape to a new spot without changing which way it "faces."

The Fix: Corner Geometry. Pick the point furthest from the origin (the top-left corner of the F). In a vertical reflection, that point must land at the same height but on the far right. The internal "arms" must now point toward the center if they started pointing away.
