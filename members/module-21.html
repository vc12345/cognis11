Module 21: Venn Diagrams (2 Circles)

Phase 1: The X-Ray

A Venn Diagram is a visual map of Mutual Properties. The point where circles overlap is the Intersection ($A \cap B$). This area contains items that satisfy both sets simultaneously. The "Logical Trap" in 11+ exams is counting these items twice. To solve any Venn puzzle, you must subtract the Intersection from the Group Totals to find the "Only" remainders.

Advanced Year 2 "In a class of 12 footballers, 3 also play rugby. How many play football ONLY?"
Average Year 4 "Group A = 20, Group B = 15. The intersection is 5. Find the total Union (everyone in both circles)."
Advanced Year 5 "Out of 40 students, 25 like Art and 20 like Music. Calculate the minimum Intersection size."

Phase 2: The Anatomy

The Overlap Shift. Watch what happens to the values when the groups interact. Notice how the "Total" remains the same, but the "Unique" counts must decrease to account for the shared items.

Phase 4: The Saboteur

The "Blind Addition" Trap: Adding the two totals together to find the total population without considering the overlap. (e.g. 15 Footballers + 10 Swimmers = 25 people). If 5 people do both, your total is wrong by exactly 5.

The Fix: Use the Set Subtraction Rule. Before calculating the total (Union), you must subtract the overlap from the totals. The formula is: $Total = (A - Intersection) + (B - Intersection) + Intersection$. This ensures every individual is counted exactly once.
