Module 33: Hidden Words

Phase 1: The X-Ray

Hidden word puzzles test Boundary Dissolution. In standard reading, we see spaces as walls that define where words begin and end. To find a hidden word, you must ignore these structural walls and treat the sentence as a continuous stream of characters. Most hidden words are "Bridge Words"—they exist across the seam where two words meet.

Advanced Year 2 "Find the 3-letter word hidden in the phrase: 'THE ATOM'."
Average Year 4 "Find the 4-letter word hidden in the phrase: 'FISH ARE DEEP'."
Advanced Year 5 "Find the 4-letter word hidden in the phrase: 'MADE A GOAL'."

Phase 4: The Saboteur

The "Intra-Word" Trap: Searching for a smaller word entirely *inside* one of the existing words (e.g., finding the word "CAT" inside "SCATTER"). In 11+ exams, the hidden word almost never sits comfortably inside another word.

The Fix: Use Seam Searching. Physically place your finger or a pen over the space between two words. The hidden word will nearly always be formed by taking the last few letters of one word and the first few letters of the next. Focus 100% of your attention on the junctions.
