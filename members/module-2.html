Module 2: Rounding & Estimation

THE "BOUNDARY" PRINCIPLE

Phase 1: The X-Ray

A "Type 1" thinker just looks at the whole number. A "Type 2" thinker identifies the Critical Digit (the one to the right of the rounding place) to decide whether to push the value up or let it rest.

Exam Wording Patterns:
1. "Round 456 to the nearest hundred."
2. "Give your answer to 1 decimal place."
3. "Estimate the total cost by rounding each item to the nearest pound."

Phase 2: The Anatomy

The Rule: If the digit is **5 or more**, round up. If it is **4 or less**, round down (keep the digit the same).
