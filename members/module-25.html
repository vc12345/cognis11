Module 25: Combinatorics (Handshakes)

Phase 1: The X-Ray

Combinatorics is the math of counting possibilities. In "Handshake" problems, every person interacts with every other person once. This requires us to eliminate self-interactions and duplicated pairings.

Advanced Year 2If 4 friends high-five each other once, how many high-fives happen?
Average Year 48 teams play in a league. Every team plays every other team once. How many games total?
Advanced Year 5In a room, 45 handshakes took place. How many people were there?

Phase 4: The Saboteur

The "Reflection" Trap: If there are 10 people, $10 \times 9 = 90$ counts every handshake twice (A shaking B, and B shaking A).

The Fix: Look at the matrix above. The top-right triangle is a perfect mirror of the bottom-left. To get the unique handshakes, we must discard one half of the mirror. That is why we divide by 2.
