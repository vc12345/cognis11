Module 13: Train & Tunnel

Phase 1: The X-Ray

A train is a "moving object with length." To pass an object completely, the train's nose must travel the length of the object plus the length of the train itself.

Advanced Year 2 "A train 50m long passes a signal post. How far must the nose travel from touching the post to the tail clearing it?"
Average Year 4 "A 100m train travels through a 300m tunnel. What is the total distance covered to clear the tunnel?"
Advanced Year 5 "A 200m train passes a 400m platform in 30 seconds. Calculate the speed of the train in m/s."

Phase 2: The Anatomy

Watch the Total Distance. The clock doesn't stop when the nose hits the exit; it stops when the tail clears the tunnel.

Phase 4: The Saboteur

The "Tail-End" Trap: Most students only use the tunnel length as the distance (e.g. 400m). If you do this, the train is still halfway inside the tunnel when your calculation "ends."

The Fix: Imagine the train is a piece of string. To pull a 2m string through a 4m tube, you have to pull the string a total of 6m before it is completely out. Always add: Train Length + Object Length.
