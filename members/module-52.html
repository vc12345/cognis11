Module 52: Explicit Retrieval

Phase 1: The X-Ray

Explicit Retrieval is the logic of surface-level facts. Unlike inference, which asks you to guess feelings, retrieval asks you to find data that is physically written on the page. Success depends on treating the text as a database rather than a story.

Advanced Year 2 The kitten found a silver bell in the garden.

Q: What color was the bell?
Average Year 4 The expedition was led by Captain Vance during the winter of 1892.

Q: Who was the leader of the expedition?
Advanced Year 5 The compound, C-42, remained stable at exactly -15 degrees.

Q: At what temperature was the compound stable?

Phase 2: The Anatomy

Parent Instruction: Explain to your child that they are looking for a "twin." If the question asks for a Name, their eyes should skip over all small words and only stop at words starting with Capital Letters.

Phase 4: The Saboteur

The Trap

Reading the text for enjoyment. Many students spend 2 minutes reading the whole story before looking at the question. This wastes valuable time and fills the brain with unnecessary details.

The Fix

Sacrificial Scanning. Read the question first. Identify the "Hook" word (like a date or name). Use your finger to scan the text in a Z-shape until you hit the "Anchor." Extract the fact and move on immediately.
