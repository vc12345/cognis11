Module 41: Logical Ordering

Phase 1: The X-Ray

In logical ordering, we use clues to eliminate impossible sequences. By visualizing the items as color-coded tokens, we can systematically cross out any arrangement where the "Stronger" item is to the left of the "Weaker" one. Success comes from identifying the clues that narrow the field without over-relying on memory.

Phase 4: The Saboteur

The Trap: Fixing an item in "First Place" as soon as a clue mentions it is "stronger," without checking if there is another item stronger still.

The Fix: Permutation Elimination. Instead of guessing, look at the full "Possibility Space." Cross out every row where the Clue Rule is broken. The rows that aren't crossed out are your only remaining possibilities.
