Module 43: Reflection Logic

Phase 1: The X-Ray

Reflection is a "Mirror Image" operation. Unlike rotation, it creates a version of the shape that cannot be reached by spinning. The distance from the mirror line to the "Original" must be exactly the same as the distance to the "Reflection."

Advanced Year 2Reflect a square across a vertical axis.
Average Year 4Reflect an asymmetric flag horizontally.
Advanced Year 5Reflect a shape across a diagonal line ($y=x$).

Phase 4: The Saboteur

The Trap: The "Slide" Trap. Students often just move the shape to the other side of the line without flipping it (Translation instead of Reflection).

The Fix: The "Finger Test." If you put your finger on a point of the original, it should be the same distance from the mirror line as the corresponding point on the reflection. If one points left, the other MUST point right.
