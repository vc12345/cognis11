Module 9: Average Speed

THE "RATIO OF CHANGE" PRINCIPLE

Phase 1: The X-Ray

A "Type 1" thinker sees two numbers and wants to multiply them. A "Type 2" thinker understands that speed is a description of how much distance is covered per 1 unit of time.

Exam Wording Patterns:
1. "A car travels 150 miles in 3 hours. What is its speed?"
2. "Calculate the average speed of a cyclist who covers 40km in 2 hours."
3. "A plane flies from London to Paris (340 miles) in 45 minutes. Find the mph."

Phase 2: The Anatomy

Speed is the "Bridge" between Distance and Time. You can use the DST Triangle to decide which operation to use.

[Image of the Distance Speed Time triangle formula]
The Formula: Speed = Distance ÷ Time.
If you have miles and hours, your answer will be in Miles Per Hour (mph).
