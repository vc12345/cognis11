Module 6: Percentage Increase & Decrease

Phase 1: The X-Ray

Mastering percentages means moving away from "finding 10% and adding it." We use Multipliers to jump straight to the answer in one step.

Adv Year 2"A £20 teddy is on sale with 10% off. How much money is taken off the price?"
Avg Year 4"A bag of 40 marbles gets a '20% extra free' bonus. How many marbles are in the bag now?"
Adv Year 5"A shop increases all prices by 15%. If a game cost £40, what is the new price? Show the multiplier used."

Phase 4: The Saboteur

The "Literal Addition" Trap: If a price is £50 and increases by 10%, students often just add the number 10 ($50 + 10 = £60$).

The Fix: Percentages are fractions of a specific amount. Always turn the percentage into a decimal multiplier ($1.1$) before touching the original number.
