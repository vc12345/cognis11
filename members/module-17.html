Module 17: Reverse Operations

Phase 1: The X-Ray

Finding a starting number is like retracing your steps. To find where you began, you must walk backwards from the end, performing the exact opposite of every instruction in the opposite order.

Advanced Year 2 "I think of a number. I add 8 and get 20. What was my starting number?"
Average Year 4 "I multiply a number by 4, then subtract 6. The final result is 30. What was the number?"
Advanced Year 5 "I square a number, add 5, then divide the result by 3 to get 10. Find the original number."

Phase 2: The Anatomy

Phase 4: The Saboteur

The "Clockwise" Trap: Attempting to reverse the first instruction before the last one, just because it was written first in the sentence.

The Fix: Use "Last In, First Out" (LIFO). Think of putting on socks, then shoes. To reverse the process, you must take off the shoes first! Always start from the final result.
