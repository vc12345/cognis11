Module 31: Vowel/Consonant Ciphers

Phase 1: The X-Ray

In advanced coding, the rule depends on the property of the letter. Vowels (A, E, I, O, U) and Consonants are treated as separate groups. You must categorize every letter through a mental gatekeeper before applying its specific transformation rule.

Advanced Year 2 "If Vowels shift +1 and Consonants stay the same, what is the code for the word 'BAT'?"
Average Year 4 "Vowels shift +2, Consonants shift -1. Find the code for the word 'DOG'."
Advanced Year 5 "A word is encoded using split rules to become 'QMANFU'. Decode it back to 'PLANET'."

Phase 4: The Saboteur

The "One-Track" Trap: Students often apply the first rule they read to the entire word, forgetting that the rule "re-evaluates" itself at every single letter.

The Fix: Use Property Tagging. Before shifting anything, physically or mentally "tag" every letter as a V (Vowel) or C (Consonant). Only once the word is mapped should you apply the math. If you don't tag first, you will miss the pivot point where the rule flips.
