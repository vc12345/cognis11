The Origin of Cognis11

Prepared for the Deep Understander.

Standard 11+ preparation serves the pattern-matcher. We built Cognis11 for the child who refuses to move until they truly understand.

The 11+ industry is built around a single, flawed assumption: that more volume produces better results. For many children, this holds true. But for a specific type of mind—the "Deep Understander"—high-volume drilling is a recipe for frustration and performance plateaus.

These children don't lack ability. They lack an entry point. They are the students who get everything right at home but "freeze" or run out of time in a mock. They are reasoners being asked to behave like calculators.

"At Cognis11, we don't believe the preparation is failing because the child isn't working hard enough. It's failing because it's pointed at the wrong cognitive process."

The Founding Intersection

Cognis11 was born from the collision of two distinct professional worlds.

Product Strategy & Data

Our methodology was shaped by a decade of leading data strategy for multi-million user digital platforms. This background in quantitative analysis and decision-making under pressure allowed us to deconstruct the 11+ curriculum into a finite set of logical "short-circuits." We treated the exam not as a test of knowledge, but as a system of structures to be audited.

Cognitive Neuroscience

The research integrity of Cognis11 is anchored by academic experience at institutions like Cambridge and NYU. By investigating how the brain processes conscious and unconscious information, our research lead identified the specific mechanism needed to bridge the gap between deep understanding and competitive speed.

The SBI Pivot

For over thirty years, Schema-Based Instruction (SBI) has been a cornerstone of cognitive psychology. Historically, it has been used as a remedial safety net to help children who struggle with basic math by giving them a way to classify problems.

At Cognis11, we’ve done something different.

We have taken this established, evidence-based mechanism and pointed it in a novel direction: Performance Optimization. We aren't using SBI to catch up; we are using it to unlock the ceiling for high-achieving reasoners. By giving "Deep Understanders" the X-Ray vision to classify a problem structure in seconds, we eliminate the cognitive load that causes them to slow down.
