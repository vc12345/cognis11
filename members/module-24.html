Module 24: The Mean Bridge

Phase 1: The X-Ray

Topic Logic: Finding a missing value in an average problem is about "Total Awareness." A Mean is just a shorthand for a Grand Total. To find the missing piece, you must build the "Bridge" between the Total you have and the Total you need.

Advanced Year 2The mean of 3 numbers is 5. Two numbers are 4 and 6. What is the 3rd?
Average Year 4Sarah scored 12, 15, and 10 in three tests. What must she score in the 4th test to have a mean of 13?
Advanced Year 5The mean weight of 5 boys is 42kg. A 6th boy joins, and the mean becomes 43kg. Find the new boy's weight.

Phase 4: The Saboteur

The "One-Step" Trap: Trying to solve the problem by only looking at the numbers provided.

The Fix: You cannot find the missing part until you know the size of the whole. Always multiply the Target Mean by the New Count first to find the "Target Sum." Once you have the destination, the starting point (Current Sum) tells you exactly how far you have to go.
