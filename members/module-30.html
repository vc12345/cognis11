Module 30: Relative Letter Shift

Phase 1: The X-Ray

Relative letter shifting (often called the Caesar Cipher) is a logic puzzle where each letter is substituted for another based on its distance from the original. It requires Pattern Persistence: once the "Key" or "Offset" is identified, it must be applied with perfect consistency across every character in the string to reveal the hidden message.

Advanced Year 2 "If the word 'CAT' is coded as 'DBU', what is the shift? Use that same pattern to code the word 'DOG'."
Average Year 4 "In a secret code, 'FISH' becomes 'HKUJ'. Find the key and decode the mystery word 'BIRD'."
Advanced Year 5 "The code is reversed (A=Z, B=Y). If the message is 'BCZ', what was the original word?"

Phase 2: The Anatomy

Phase 4: The Saboteur

The "Static Alphabet" Trap: Assuming the alphabet is a flat line that stops at 'Z'. Students often get stuck when a shift moves past the end of the sequence.

The Fix: Use the Loop Principle. Treat the alphabet like a 26-hour clock face. If you are at 'Z' and need to shift forward 2, you don't stop; you "wrap around" back to 'A' and then 'B'. Always visualize the alphabet as a continuous ring to solve complex offsets.
