Module 11: Average Speed

Phase 1: The X-Ray

Average speed is not the average of two speeds. It is the Total Story of a journey. You must collapse every segment into one single distance and one single time.

Advanced Year 2 "A car travels 15 miles in the morning and 25 miles in the afternoon. The trip took 2 hours. What was the average speed?"
Average Year 4 "A cyclist travels 20km at 10km/h, then another 20km at 20km/h. What is the average speed for the whole trip?"
Advanced Year 5 "A plane flies 600 miles at 300mph, then 400 miles at 200mph. Calculate the average speed for the 1000-mile flight."

Phase 2: The Anatomy

Phase 4: The Saboteur

The "Mean Speed" Trap: If a car travels at 40mph then 60mph, many students assume the average is 50mph.

The Fix: This only works if the times spent at each speed are exactly the same. Since journey times usually vary, you must always find Total Distance and Total Time first.
