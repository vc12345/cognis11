Module 25: Reflection

THE "MIRROR" PRINCIPLE

Phase 1: The X-Ray

A "Type 1" thinker slides the shape (Translation). A "Type 2" thinker flips the shape. In a reflection, the image is **reversed** and the **same distance** from the mirror line as the original.

Phase 2: The Anatomy

[Image showing a complex shape being reflected across a horizontal axis to demonstrate the flip and equidistant points]
Key Check: Pick a corner on the original. Count the squares to the mirror line. The same corner on the reflection MUST be the same number of squares away on the other side.

Phase 3: The Engine

Toggle the mirror axis to see how the reflection "flips."

Phase 4: The Saboteur

The "Slide" Trap: Drawing the exact same shape on the other side of the line without flipping it. If you can't "fold" the paper along the mirror line and have the shapes match, it's a slide, not a reflection.
