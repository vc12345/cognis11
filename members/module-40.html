Module 40: Odd One Out

Phase 1: The X-Ray

Odd One Out is a test of Threshold Categorization. Success does not require reading all five words. By processing words from left to right, you can establish a "Majority Rule" as soon as you identify a shared property between any two words. Once the rule is set, the first word that breaks it is the outlier. Stop processing immediately to save time.

Advanced Year 2 "Apple, Banana, Carrot, Grape, Pear." (Identifying fruit vs vegetable).
Average Year 4 "Silver, Gold, Copper, Iron, Wood." (Identifying metals vs non-metals).
Advanced Year 5 "Brave, Bold, Fearless, Reckless, Courageous." (Identifying positive trait vs negative trait).

Phase 4: The Saboteur

The "Linear Scan" Trap: Reading all 5 words before starting to categorize. Most students treat the set as a single unit. This wastes 5–10 seconds per question on words that don't change the answer.

The Fix: Use the 2+1 Rule. If Word 1 and Word 2 match, the category is set. If Word 3 is different, it is the answer. If Word 1 and 2 are different, Word 3 acts as the tie-breaker. The moment you have a 2:1 ratio, the logic is closed. Stop scanning.
