Phase 4: The Saboteur

The "Linear" Trap: Most students read equations left-to-right like a story.
Example: 2 + 3^2. A "Type 1" thinker says 2+3=5, then 5 squared = 25.

The Fix: Roots and Powers are "heavy" operators. They must be solved before addition or subtraction can happen. In 2 + 3^2, the 9 is solved first, making the weight 11.
