Module 42: Rotational Invariance

Phase 1: The X-Ray

Rotational Invariance is the logic of Mental Manipulation. In Non-Verbal Reasoning, a shape is identical regardless of its orientation. Success depends on picking a unique feature (an Anchor) and verifying its relationship to the rest of the shape. If the anchor moves from the "right side" to the "left side" relative to the center, it is a reflection, not a rotation.

Average Year 4 "Distinguish between a turned 'L' shape and its mirrored flip."
Advanced Year 5 "Identify the complex polygon after a 135° anti-clockwise rotation."

Phase 4: The Saboteur

The "Mirror" Trap: Confusing a rotation with a Reflection. A rotation can be undone by turning the paper; a reflection (a flip) can only be undone by looking in a mirror. Distractors often use the "flipped" version at the correct angle.

The Fix: Use the Clockwise Rule. Mentally trace the features of the shape in a circle. In a rotation, the sequence of details (Pole → Arm → Anchor) remains identical. In a reflection, the order reverses. If the sequence breaks, it is a decoy.
