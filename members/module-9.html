Module 9: Sequential Fractions

Phase 1: The X-Ray

The "Remainder" is the most important word in word problems. It triggers a Reset—the leftover amount becomes the new "Whole" for the next calculation.

Adv Year 2"Tom has 12 sweets. He eats 2. He gives half of what is left to Sue. How many does Sue get?"
Avg Year 4"A pizza has 12 slices. Dad eats $\frac{1}{3}$. Then Mom eats $\frac{1}{2}$ of the remainder. How many slices are left?"
Adv Year 5"A traveler has £600. They spend $\frac{1}{4}$ on a hotel and $\frac{2}{5}$ of the remainder on food. How much do they have left?"

Phase 4: The Saboteur

The "Totalist" Trap: Students often calculate both fractions from the original total (e.g., $\frac{1}{4}$ of 600 and $\frac{2}{5}$ of 600).

The Fix: Circle the word "Remainder". It tells you that the first amount is GONE. You are now working with a smaller number.
