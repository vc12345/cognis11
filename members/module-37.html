Phase 4: The Saboteur

The "First Word" Bias: Spotting a letter that works for the first word and immediately assuming it's the answer without checking the others. For example, in RA_N, you might see "RAN" and stop, but the letter "N" won't work for the other words in the set.

The Fix: Use Total Validation. A candidate letter is only a "possibility" until it has been physically tested against all three words. If word 3 fails, the letter is a decoy. Always cycle through the entire set before selecting your final answer.
