The Cognis Origin

Preparation for the Deep Understander.

We exist because high-achieving children are often the most poorly served by standard 11+ preparation.

The 11+ industry is built around a single assumption: that more practice produces better results. For many children, that is true. But for a specific type of child—one who needs to construct a logical model before they can act—repetitive drilling often leads to frustration and plateauing scores.

These "Deep Understanders" consistently get questions right in relaxed conditions, but struggle when the clock starts. Tutors usually prescribe more papers, but the gap doesn't close because the preparation was designed for a different type of mind.

"The preparation isn't failing because the child isn't working hard enough. It's failing because it's asking a reasoner to behave like a pattern-matcher."

Cognis 11+ was founded to bridge this gap by applying **Schema-Based Instruction (SBI)** to high-performance preparation. We focus on training the brain to classify the "structure" of a problem before attempting the math. When a child understands the category, speed follows naturally.

The science of Schema

Schema-Based Instruction is not a new theory; it is an established pillar of cognitive psychology studied for over three decades. It is specifically designed to help students identify the underlying "schema" of a problem—the logical skeleton—independent of the numbers involved.

At Cognis, we’ve taken this validated academic mechanism and pointed it at the 11+. We don't just give children questions; we give them the "X-Ray" vision required to see the logic behind the questions.
