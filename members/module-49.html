Module 49: Rotated Cubes

Phase 1: The X-Ray

A cube has 6 faces, but you only see 3 at a time. To solve rotation puzzles, pick one symbol and treat it as the "Top." Then, track where the other two symbols "live" relative to it. If the Circle is to the right of the Star on the Target, it MUST be to the right of the Star on the Answer (assuming the Star is oriented the same way).

Advanced Year 2

Identify a cube after one 90° turn to the right.

Average Year 4

Spot the "Impossible Neighbor" (a face that should be opposite but is shown touching).

Advanced Year 5

Analyze orientation: Does the arrow point toward or away from the adjacent dot?

Phase 4: The Saboteur

The Trap: The "Impossible Neighbor." Picking an answer where two symbols are touching, even though you know from the net (or a previous view) that they are **Opposites**.

The Fix: Use the "Elimination of Opposites." If the Star and the Circle are opposites, any option showing both at once is instantly wrong. Cross it out immediately and don't waste time checking its rotation.
