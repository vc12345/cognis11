Module 2: Shared Ratios (Total Known)

Phase 1: The X-Ray

In a ratio, every "part" is the same size. To share a total, you must find out how many parts there are in total first. Think of it as a chocolate bar: how many squares do we have to snap off?

The "Single Part" Trap: Students often divide the total by only one side of the ratio (e.g., $100 \div 3$). Stop! You must divide by the sum of the ratio parts because the total covers the whole bar, not just one person's share.
