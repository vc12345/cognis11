Module 3: Shared Ratios (The Gap)

Phase 1: The X-Ray

In these questions, you aren't given the total. You are given the Difference (The Gap) between two people. You must find how many "extra parts" create that gap.

Advanced Year 2 "Money is shared in the ratio 3:1. If Person A has £10 more than Person B, how much does B have?"
Average Year 4 "The ratio of boys to girls is 5:2. There are 15 more boys than girls. Calculate the number of girls."
Advanced Year 5 "A recipe uses flour and sugar in the ratio 7:3. If I use 200g more flour than sugar, what is the total mass of the mixture?"

Phase 4: The Saboteur

The "Blind Total" Trap: Students often see a number and a ratio and immediately add the ratio parts (e.g. 5+3=8) to divide.

The Fix: Always identify the "Anchor." If the number is a Difference, you must find the Difference in Parts (5 - 3 = 2). Divide the gap value by the gap parts.
