Module 13: Solving for X

Phase 1: The X-Ray

An equation is a perfectly balanced seesaw. To find out what $x$ weighs, you must strip away the numbers. But the Golden Rule applies: Whatever you do to the left, you must do to the right.

Entry"$x + 7 = 15$. Find the value of $x$."
Standard"$3x + 4 = 19$. Solve for $x$."
Advanced"I think of a number, multiply it by 4, and add 6. The answer is 34. What was my number?"

Phase 4: The Saboteur

The "Magic Moving" Trap: Students often just move a '+4' to the other side without changing its sign, ending up with $2x = 10 + 4$.

The Fix: Numbers don't jump the equals sign. You are physically subtracting 4 from both sides to cancel it out on the left. If you add 4 blocks to the right instead of removing them, the scale crashes.
