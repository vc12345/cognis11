Module 47: Valid Cube Nets

Phase 1: The X-Ray

A cube net is a flat "blueprint" of a 3D solid. The most critical rule for 11+ success is identifying Opposite Pairs. Since opposite faces never share an edge on a folded cube, finding them allows you to eliminate decoy answers immediately. In a 1-4-1 net, any two faces separated by one square in a straight line are opposites.

Phase 4: The Saboteur

The Trap: The "Touching Corner" Illusion. Many students assume that if two squares share a corner on the flat net, they will touch on the cube.

The Fix: The Opposite Shield. Identify your three opposite pairs first. If an answer choice shows two opposites sharing an edge, that choice is a lie. Cross it out and don't look back.
