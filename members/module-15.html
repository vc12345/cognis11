Module 15: Time Conversions

Phase 1: The X-Ray

Time does not use the standard decimal system (Base-10). It uses Base-60. The core logic is managing the "Reset Point" where 60 minutes transform into 1 hour.

Advanced Year 2 "A baker leaves a cake in the oven for 85 minutes. Convert this into hours and minutes."
Average Year 4 "A flight from London to Paris takes 1 hour and 45 minutes. How many minutes is this in total?"
Advanced Year 5 "A marathon runner finishes in 215 minutes. Express their time in hours and minutes."

Phase 4: The Saboteur

The "Decimal" Trap: Mistakenly thinking that 1.5 hours is 1 hour and 50 minutes because 5 is half of 10.

The Fix: Always remember that in time, 0.5 = 30. Because the "container" is 60 minutes wide, half of the container is 30. Never use 100 as your boundary!
