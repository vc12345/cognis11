Module 26: Area/Perimeter Invariance

Phase 1: The X-Ray

Perimeter is 1-dimensional length (the "fence"); Area is 2-dimensional space (the "grass"). While a boundary may remain a fixed length, the internal capacity fluctuates. A Square configuration always maximizes the internal area for any given perimeter.

Advanced Year 2 "A garden has a 12m fence. If it is built as a square, what is the internal area in square metres?"
Average Year 4 "Rectangle A is 8cm x 2cm. Rectangle B is 5cm x 5cm. They both have a 20cm perimeter, but which has the larger area?"
Advanced Year 5 "A piece of wire is 40cm long. Compare the area created when bent into a 15x5 rectangle versus a 10x10 square."

Phase 4: The Saboteur

The "Fixed Area" Trap: Mistakenly believing that because the perimeter (the length of the string) is 40cm, any rectangle you make with it will have the same amount of space inside.

The Fix: Remember the Balance Principle. If you have a perimeter of 20, a long thin 9x1 rectangle only has an area of 9. But a balanced 5x5 square has an area of 25. To get the most space, keep the sides as equal as possible.
