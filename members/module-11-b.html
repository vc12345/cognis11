B1.2: Average Speed — The Tug-of-War

Phase 1: The X-Ray

Time is like gravity. It pulls the Average Speed toward whichever speed lasted the longest. We call this the Tug-of-War.

Strategy A Spent 5 hours walking? The average will be "walking speed."
Strategy B Spent 1 minute sprinting? The sprint speed is too weak to move the average.
The Goal Look for the "Time Winner" to guess where the average will land.

Phase 2: The Anatomy

Notice which block is wider. That leg of the journey took more time, so it "wins" the tug-of-war.

SLOW WALK (4 HOURS)
SPRINT (1 HR)

The "Slow" side is heavier. It pulls the average down!

Phase 3: The Engine

→ Slow Time: 4.00 hrs (HEAVY)
→ Fast Time: 1.00 hrs (LIGHT)
→ Result: Average is stuck near the slow speed.
AVERAGE: Pulled by Gravity

Phase 4: The Saboteur

The "Middle-Man" Trap

If a car travels at 20mph then 60mph, bright children will shout "40mph!" because it's exactly in the middle. They forget that the clock isn't usually a tie.

The Fix: Ask your child: "Is the time a perfect tie?" If they spent 3 hours going slow and 1 hour going fast, the answer cannot be the middle number. It must be pulled toward the slow side.
