Module 54: Tone & Purpose

Phase 1: The X-Ray

Tone is the "Emotional Temperature" of a text (how the author feels). Purpose is the "Mechanical Goal" (why the author is writing). By changing a few key adjectives, an author can change an informative text into a sarcastic or aggressive one.

Advanced Year 2"Eat your greens now, or no pudding!"

Tone: Stern / Bossy
Purpose: To Instruct
Average Year 4"The sun's golden rays kissed the calm, blue ocean."

Tone: Peaceful / Descriptive
Purpose: To Paint a Picture
Advanced Year 5"The 'new' law is as effective as a paper umbrella in a storm."

Tone: Cynical / Sarcastic
Purpose: To Ridicule

Phase 2: The Anatomy

Parent Instruction: Ask your child to look for "Feeling Words." If you remove the adjectives, is the sentence still "mean" or "happy"? Usually, the tone disappears when the adjectives are removed.

Phase 4: The Saboteur

The Trap

The Subject Trap. Students often think the tone is "sad" just because the topic is sad (like a rainy day). They react to the event, not the author's voice.

The Fix

The Adjective Audit. Ignore what is happening and look only at the adjectives. If a sad event is described with cold, scientific words, the tone is Clinical, not sad.
