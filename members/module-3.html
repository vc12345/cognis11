Module 3: Shared Ratios (The Gap)

Phase 1: The X-Ray

In many 11+ questions, the examiner won't tell you the "Total." Instead, they'll tell you the Difference (the gap) between two people. The secret weapon here is finding out how many Extra Boxes create that gap. If your child can see that "£20 more" is caused by "2 extra boxes," they've already won the points.

The Pocket Money "Money shared 3:1. Person A has £10 more than B. How much does B have?"
The Classroom "Ratio of boys to girls is 5:2. There are 15 more boys. How many girls?"
The Recipe Mass "Flour and sugar are 7:3. If there's 200g more flour, find the total weight."

Phase 4: The Saboteur

The "Total Sum" Trap: Children often go on autopilot and add the ratio parts together (e.g., 5 + 3 = 8) to divide the number. This is a disaster if the number is actually a Difference. It makes every box look too small.

The Fix: The "Minus First" Rule. Remind your child: "If you see words like 'more than,' 'less than,' or 'difference,' you must subtract the ratio parts first." (5 - 3 = 2). The "Gap Value" only lives in the "Gap Parts."
