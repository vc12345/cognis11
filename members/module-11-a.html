B1.1: Average Speed — The Big Squash

Phase 1: The X-Ray (The "Fair Share" Logic)

Forget the word "Average." It sounds like homework. Instead, think of "The Fair Share."

If a car travels 20 miles in 2 hours, we just want to know: "If the miles were shared out perfectly, how many miles would sit in each 1-hour box?"

1. The Pile Put all the miles into one big "Distance Pile."
2. The Boxes Count how many "Hour Boxes" you have in your hand.
3. The Share Drop the miles into the boxes equally. That's your speed!

Phase 2: The Anatomy (The Journey Merger)

Point to the Orange Leg and the Blue Leg. We are going to "squash" them into one big road.

10 miles
20 miles
↓ SQUASHED INTO ↓
TOTAL ROAD: 30 MILES

Success is realizing that 10 + 20 is the only thing that matters right now.

Phase 3: The Engine (The Sharing Machine)

CHANGE THE TOTALS TO SEE THE SHARE:

40 miles
2 hours
[SYSTEM STATUS: CALCULATING PACE]
→ Gathering all miles: 40
→ Preparing 2 hour-boxes...
→ Sharing 40 into 2 groups...
RESULT: 20 MILES PER HOUR

Phase 4: The Saboteur

The "Double-Counting" Trap

If you ask: "A car goes 10 miles in 1 hour, and 10 miles in 1 hour. What is the average speed?", bright kids often add the hours and the miles, but then get confused and think the speed is "20."

The Fix: Use the "Box Rule." Tell them: "We only want to know how many miles are in ONE box. If 2 boxes have 20 miles, how many in just one?" This forces them to divide without realizing they are doing "hard math."
