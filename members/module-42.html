Module 42: Rotational Invariance

Phase 1: The X-Ray

A shape remains identical even if it is spinning. The key to solving "Which shape is the same?" is Vertex Tracking—picking a specific feature (like a dot or a notch) and checking if its position relative to the rest of the shape remains constant as it turns.

Phase 4: The Saboteur

The Trap: Confusing a Rotation with a Reflection. A rotation can be undone by spinning the page; a reflection (flip) can only be undone by looking at it in a mirror.

The Fix: Use the "Clockwise Rule." Look at the sequence of features going clockwise around the shape. In a rotation, the order stays the same. In a reflection, the order reverses.
