Module 3: Shared Ratios (The Gap)

Phase 1: The X-Ray

In 11+ ratio questions, the value you are given isn't always the total. Often, you are given the Difference (the gap) between two shares. Success depends on identifying how many "extra parts" create that specific gap. To solve these, you must find the value of one part by dividing the gap amount by the Difference in Parts.

Advanced Year 2 "Money is shared in the ratio 3:1. If Person A has £10 more than Person B, how much does B have?"
Average Year 4 "The ratio of boys to girls is 5:2. There are 15 more boys than girls. Calculate the number of girls."
Advanced Year 5 "A recipe uses flour and sugar in the ratio 7:3. If there is 200g more flour than sugar, find the total mass."

Phase 4: The Saboteur

The "Blind Total" Trap: Mistakenly adding the ratio parts (e.g. 5 + 3 = 8) and dividing the number by that sum. This only works if the number is the Total. If the number is a Difference, your answer will be way too small.

The Fix: Always identify the Anchor. If the question says "more than," "less than," or "the difference is," you must find the Difference in Parts first (e.g. 5 - 3 = 2). Divide the gap value only by the gap parts.
