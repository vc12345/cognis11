Module 5: Direct Proportion

Phase 1: The X-Ray

Direct Proportion is about things moving in Perfect Lockstep. If you buy twice as many stickers, you pay twice as much money. The most bulletproof strategy for the 11+ is the Power of One (the Unitary Method): always find the cost or weight of exactly one item first, then multiply to get your final answer.

The Sticker Shop "If 2 stickers cost 10p, how much do 6 stickers cost?"
The Baker's Kitchen "A recipe for 4 people uses 300g of sugar. How much for 10 people?"
The Map Reader "On a 1:25,000 scale map, a road is 8cm long. What is the real length in km?"

Phase 4: The Saboteur

The "Just Add Some" Trap: If a recipe for 4 people needs 10g of salt, and we need to feed 6 people, a child might think: "That's 2 more people, so I'll just add 2g of salt." (Total 12g).

The Fix: The "Taste Test." Ask your child: "If we just add 2g, will it still taste the same, or will it be too weak?" Remind them that proportion is a Times Table Game, never an addition game. You must multiply the salt by the same "growth factor" as the people.
