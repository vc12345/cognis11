Module 16: The "More Than" Trap

Phase 1: The X-Ray

This trap occurs when you are given a Combined Total and the Gap (difference) between two people. Your brain wants to divide by 2 immediately—but that is a mistake.

Advanced Year 2 "Two friends have 12 stickers. Sarah has 4 more than Tom. How many stickers does Tom have?"
Average Year 4 "A tablet and a case cost £250. The tablet is £200 more than the case. Calculate the cost of the case."
Advanced Year 5 "The combined age of a father and son is 50. The father is 26 years older than the son. How old is the son?"

Phase 2: The Anatomy

To solve this, you must level the playing field. Subtract the difference to create two identical blocks, then share.

Phase 4: The Saboteur

The "Impulse Split" Trap: Most students take the total and divide it by 2 first (e.g., 100 ÷ 2 = 50), then add the difference. If you do this, your total will no longer be 100!

The Fix: Always "Snip" the difference off the total first. Once you have removed the extra part, you have two equal shares. Divide that remainder by 2, then give the "extra" back to the larger person.
