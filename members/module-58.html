Module 58: Punctuation Mechanics

Phase 1: The X-Ray

Punctuation is the Traffic Control of language. A Semicolon (;) links two independent thoughts. A Colon (:) introduces a result or a list. A Comma (,) is for pausing, but using it to join two sentences is a "Comma Splice" error.

Advanced Year 2I like apples, oranges, and pears.

Rule: Serial Commas.
Target: Listing items.
Average Year 4It was raining; therefore, we stayed inside.

Rule: Semicolon.
Target: Joining two full sentences.
Advanced Year 5The results were clear: we had won.

Rule: Colon.
Target: Introducing a reveal.

Phase 2: The Anatomy

Parent Instruction: Use the "Full Sentence Test." Cover the punctuation. If the words on the left and right can both stand as their own sentences, use a **Semicolon**. If the right side is a list or a summary, use a **Colon**.

Phase 4: The Saboteur

The Trap

The Comma Splice. Students often feel that if they pause while reading, they should use a comma. This leads to sentences like: "I am tired, I am going to bed." This is a grammatical error.
