Module 45: Attribute Matrices

Phase 1: The X-Ray

A matrix is a grid where items evolve based on Concurrent Rules. To solve for the missing cell, you must distinguish between the "Row Rule" (what changes horizontally) and the "Column Rule" (what changes vertically). The final answer is a fusion of both: it must satisfy the requirements of its specific row and column simultaneously.

Advanced Year 2 Row 1: Red Sq, Blue Sq, Green Sq.
Row 2: Red Tr, Blue Tr, [?].
Average Year 4 A 3x3 grid where elements rotate 90° clockwise in every successive cell.
Advanced Year 5 Size increases down columns, while color alternates across rows.

Phase 4: The Saboteur

The "One-Rule" Trap: Finding a single pattern (e.g., "the shapes are all circles") and picking an answer immediately. In 11+ exams, the answer often uses a circle distractor that has the wrong color or orientation.

The Fix: Use the Cross-Hair Method. Isolate Attribute 1 (Shape) by looking only at the rows. Isolate Attribute 2 (Color/Fill) by looking only at the columns. Write them down separately. The answer must be the perfect "sum" of your findings.
