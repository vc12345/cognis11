Module 28: Net-to-Volume

Phase 1: The X-Ray

A 3D object is a 2D surface wrapped in space. Volume is the Base Area multiplied by the Height. In a net, the 'Height' is determined by the length of the flaps that fold up perpendicular to the base.

Phase 4: The Saboteur

The "Area vs Volume" trap: The total area of the paper net is the Surface Area. The space inside the folded box is the Volume. To avoid confusion, always multiply the base dimensions first, then 'extrude' that area by the height of the side flaps.
