Module 28: Area (Rectangles & Triangles)

Phase 1: The X-Ray

Area is the space inside a shape. It is measured in square units ($cm^2$, $m^2$).

Phase 2: The Anatomy

[Image comparing the area of a rectangle ($Area = l \times w$) and a triangle ($Area = \frac{1}{2} \times b \times h$), showing the triangle as exactly half of the rectangle]
Rectangle: Base $\times$ Height
Triangle: $\frac{1}{2} \times$ Base $\times$ Height

Area: $cm^2$

Phase 4: The Saboteur

The "Half-Forgotten" Triangle: Forgetting to divide by 2 when calculating a triangle's area. A triangle is always half of a rectangle with the same base and height!
