Module 01: The Unitary Method

Phase 1: The X-Ray

The "Unitary" logic is the skeleton of all proportionality. It mandates finding the value of one before scaling to the many.

"8 identical books cost £40. Find the cost of 12 books."
"A machine makes 150 parts in 5 hours. How many in 8 hours?"
"If 20kg of soil covers 4m², how much for 10m²?"

Phase 2: The Anatomy

Visualizing the Bridge to One. The total is sliced into equal parts to find the "Unit Value".

Unit Value: £0.00

Phase 4: The Saboteur

Type 1 Memory Traps

  • The "Which Way?" Error: Dividing 5 units by £100 (0.05) instead of £100 by 5 units (£20). Type 2 Fix: Always label your answer. Does "0.05 pounds per book" pass the common-sense check?
  • The "Middle Man" Skip: Trying to jump from 5 to 12 without finding 1. Unless it's a clean multiple (like 5 to 10), skipping the "Bridge to One" usually leads to calculation fatigue and errors.
