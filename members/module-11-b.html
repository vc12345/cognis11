B1.2: Average Speed — The Tug-of-War

Phase 1: The X-Ray (The "Gravity" Secret)

In the 11+, examiners want your child to guess the Middle Number. But Average Speed has gravity!

Whichever speed you spend the most time doing is the "Winner." It pulls the average toward it like a heavy magnet.

The Long Crawl If you walk for 4 hours at 2mph, that 2mph is very "heavy."
The Short Sprint If you run for 1 minute at 20mph, that 20mph is very "light."
The Result The average will be stuck near the "heavy" walking speed.

Phase 2: The Anatomy (The Speed Scale)

Look at the Balance Scale. The "Slow" side is massive because the car stayed there for a long time. The "Fast" side is tiny.

SLOW (3hrs)
HEAVY
FAST (10min)

RESULT: The average is "pulled" to the SLOW side.

Phase 3: The Engine (The Gravity Simulator)

[TUG-OF-WAR STATUS]
→ Slow Speed Strength: 80% (WINNING)
→ Fast Speed Strength: 10% (LOSING)
→ LOGIC: The average is anchored to the slow speed.
AVERAGE PREDICTION: VERY SLOW

Phase 4: The Saboteur

The "Fair-Play" Trap

If a question says: "A plane flies at 500mph then 300mph," bright kids want to say "400mph" because it's fair. They are treating it like a tie.

The Fix: Point to the clock. Say: "Look at the time! One speed lasted much longer. In a tug-of-war, the person who pulls for longer wins. The middle number is a lie!"
