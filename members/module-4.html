Module 4: Inverse Proportion

Phase 1: The X-Ray

In most math, when one number goes up, the other follows. Inverse proportion is different—it's a See-Saw. If you double the workers, the time is cut in half. The secret is that the Total Work never changes. To solve these, your child must find that "Magic Total" first by multiplying the starting numbers together:

Workers × Time = The Magic Total (Constant)
The Tidy-Up "2 kids take 6 mins to tidy a room. How long for 3 kids?"
The Building Site "4 builders take 10 days to build a wall. How many days for 5 builders?"
The Water Pump "6 pumps empty a tank in 14 hours. 2 break down. How long for the rest?"

Phase 4: The Saboteur

The "More is More" Trap: This is the most common 11+ error. A child might think: "If 4 workers take 10 days, then 8 workers must take 20 days." They've doubled the help but increased the time!

The Fix: The "Helper Test." Ask your child: "If more friends come to help you tidy your Lego, does it take you longer or shorter?" If the answer is shorter, remind them they must Divide the Magic Total by the new number of helpers.
