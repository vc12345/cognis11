Module 43: Reflection Logic

Phase 1: The X-Ray

Reflection is a Mirror Operation that reverses the orientation (handedness) of a shape across an axis. While rotation preserves the sequence of features, reflection flips them. To solve high-level Non-Verbal Reasoning puzzles, use the Equidistant Rule: track the point furthest from the mirror—it must remain the furthest point in the reflected output, just on the opposite side.

Advanced Year 2 "Reflect a square across a vertical axis. Does it look different?"
Average Year 4 "Perform a horizontal flip on an asymmetrical 'P' shape. Which way is it facing?"
Advanced Year 5 "Identify the complex result of two consecutive diagonal reflections at 45°."

Phase 2: The Anatomy

The Mirror Axis. Pick a vertex on the shape. Measure its distance to the red dashed line. The reflection must place that vertex at the exact same distance on the opposite side. If the "arms" of the shape point away from the axis, they must point away in the result.

Phase 4: The Saboteur

The "Slide" Trap: Mistaking a reflection for a simple translation. Many students assume the shape just moves to a new quadrant without changing its orientation (how it "faces").

The Fix: Use Corner Geometry. Pick the corner furthest from the origin. In a vertical reflection, that point must land at the same height but on the far right. The internal "features" (like the arms of the F) must reverse their direction relative to the mirror.
