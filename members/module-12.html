Module 12: Relative Speed

Phase 1: The X-Ray

Relative speed determines how quickly the distance between two moving objects disappears. When objects move toward each other, they share the work of closing the gap. Success in 11+ speed problems depends on calculating the Closing Rate—the sum of both speeds—before trying to find the time of impact.

Advanced Year 2 "Two cars are 10 miles apart. Both drive at 5mph toward each other. How long until they meet?"
Average Year 4 "London and Brighton are 50km apart. A cyclist leaves London at 10km/h; a runner leaves Brighton at 15km/h. When do they pass?"
Advanced Year 5 "Train A (60mph) and Train B (90mph) are 300 miles apart. If Train B starts 30 mins late, where do they meet?"

Phase 4: The Saboteur

The "Middle Meet" Trap: Assuming two objects will always meet exactly halfway (50%) regardless of their speed. Students often instinctively divide the distance by 2.

The Fix: Use Proportional Distance. The meeting point will always be closer to the starting line of the slower object. If Object A is twice as fast as Object B, it will cover 2/3 of the distance while Object B only covers 1/3.
