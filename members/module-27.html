Module 27: Angle Logic

Phase 1: The X-Ray

Geometry is the study of Invariant Relationships. In angle logic, a single line (the transversal) cutting through parallel lines creates two families of angles. Every angle in the entire system is either equal to the others in its family, or combines with the other family to equal exactly 180°.

Advanced Year 2 "Angles on a straight line add to 180°. If one angle is 110°, find the value of its neighbor."
Average Year 4 "Parallel lines are cut by a transversal. If the top-right angle is 60°, what is the value of the 'Alternate' (Z) angle?"
Advanced Year 5 "Identify 'Co-interior' (C) angles. If one is 75°, why must the other be 105°?"

Phase 4: The Saboteur

The "Visual Parallel" Trap: Assuming lines are parallel just because they "look" parallel on the page. Students often apply Z, F, and C angle logic to lines that are slightly tilted toward each other.

The Fix: Look for Explicit Proof. You can only use parallel line logic if the question states they are parallel, or if the lines feature the standard "Arrowhead" symbols. Without those symbols, the angles at the top and bottom junctions have no relationship.
