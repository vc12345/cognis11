Module 47: Valid Cube Nets

Phase 1: The X-Ray

A cube net is a 2D blueprint of a 3D solid. The secret to mastering these in the 11+ is identifying Opposite Pairs. Because opposite faces can never touch or be visible at the same time on a folded cube, finding them allows you to eliminate decoy answers instantly. Use the Leapfrog Rule: in any straight line of three or more squares, the faces separated by exactly one square are opposites.

Advanced Year 2 Identify the "base" face if the top face is selected in a standard Cross net.
Average Year 4 Find the three opposite pairs in a 1-4-1 "T-shape" net.
Advanced Year 5 Solve the "Z-Net" where no straight line of three squares exists.

Phase 4: The Saboteur

The "Touching Corner" Trap: Assuming that if two faces share a corner on the flat net, they must touch on the cube. This is a visual illusion. Many faces that seem far apart on the net actually fold to meet at a single edge.

The Fix: Use the Opposite Shield. Don't look for what *does* touch; look for what *can't*. Find your three opposite pairs first. If a 3D cube answer shows two of those opposites sharing an edge, that answer is mathematically impossible. Cross it out immediately.
