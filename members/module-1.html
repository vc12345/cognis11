Phase 1: The X-Ray

The Unitary Method is the fundamental logic of Scaling. It collapses a complex relationship back to a single baseline (The Bridge) before rebuilding it to the target requirement.

Exam Dialects:
  • "If 6 identical crates weigh 72kg, what is the weight of 11 crates?"
  • "A car travels 150 miles on 15 liters of fuel. How far can it go on 22 liters?"
  • "The cost of 8 pens is £4.80. Calculate the cost of 5 pens."

Phase 4: The Saboteur

Type 1 Pitfalls:

  • The Inverse Slip: Dividing Items by Price (6 ÷ 60 = 0.1) instead of Price by Items (60 ÷ 6 = 10). Always ask: "Does £0.10 per item make sense?"
  • The Additive Trap: Seeing "6 items is £60, find 10 items" and thinking "It's 4 more items, so I'll add 4 to the price." 11+ logic is multiplicative, never additive.
  • The Decimal Fear: If the Bridge to One results in a decimal (e.g., 1.25), don't panic. The Unitary Method is a calculation tool, not a "whole number only" rule.
