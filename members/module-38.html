Module 38: Synonym Precision

Phase 1: The X-Ray

Synonyms are not interchangeable. Words carry Levels of Intensity. In 11+ Verbal Reasoning, choosing the correct synonym requires matching the "Volume" of the word to the drama of the sentence. A slight breeze is gentle, but a hurricane is violent. Mastering the magnitude scale allows you to filter out decoys that are technically correct but contextually "too quiet."

Phase 4: The Saboteur

The "Volume Mismatch" Trap: Choosing a synonym that is accurate but "underpowered" for the sentence. For example, describing a massive volcanic explosion as a "noisy" event instead of a "cataclysmic" one.

The Fix: Use the Drama Test. Look at the adjectives and verbs surrounding the blank. If the sentence is high-drama (e.g., using words like shattered, screamed, or instantly), you must choose the word from the extreme red end of the spectrum.
