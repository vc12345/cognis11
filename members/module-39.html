Phase 1: The X-Ray

An analogy is a logical ratio. The relationship between the first pair must be identical to the relationship between the second pair. Success requires identifying the Logical Compass—the specific direction and type of the connection.

Phase 4: The Saboteur

The "Association" Trap: Linking words because they are related, but not in the right *way*.

The Fix: Verbalize the rule as a sentence. For "Finger : Hand," the sentence is: "A finger is a part of a hand." Now test the second pair: "A toe is a part of a foot." If the sentence works for both, the analogy is solid.
