Module 32: Data Interpretation

Phase 1: The X-Ray

A "Type 1" thinker looks at a table and sees a wall of numbers. A "Type 2" thinker looks for **Intersection**. They find the right row, then the right column, and only then do they look at the number.

Phase 2: The Anatomy

In reasoning exams, the question often asks for the Difference (Subtract) or the Total (Add) between two specific intersections.

Phase 3: The Engine

Scenario: A shop sells 3 types of fruit. Use the table to answer the questions.

FruitPrice per kgStock Level
Apples£2.0040kg
Bananas£1.5060kg
Cherries£5.0010kg

Question: How much more does 1kg of Cherries cost than 1kg of Apples?

Logic: Cherries (£5.00) - Apples (£2.00) = £3.00

Phase 4: The Saboteur

The "Wrong Row" Trap: Sliding your finger across the page and accidentally picking a number from the row above or below. Use a ruler or your pen to "lock" the row!
