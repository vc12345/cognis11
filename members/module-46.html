Module 46: Series Progression

Phase 1: The X-Ray

A non-verbal series is a visual pattern that evolves across a sequence of frames. To predict the final frame, you must calculate the Delta—the specific unit of change between steps. If an object rotates 45 degrees in Frame 2, it must follow that exact mathematical vector in Frame 4. Success depends on tracking individual elements rather than the whole image.

Advanced Year 2 "A circle moves from the top-left to the bottom-right and back again. What is the pattern?"
Average Year 4 "A shape rotates 90 degrees clockwise and flips its color from black to white in each step."
Advanced Year 5 "Triple Variables: An object changes its position, its internal shading, and its orientation all at once."

Phase 4: The Saboteur

The "Visual Overload" Trap: Attempting to "guess" the final answer by staring at all moving parts simultaneously. The brain becomes overwhelmed by the noise and misses the specific Delta of the center shape.

The Fix: Use Component Tracking. Physically cover the center of the frame and track only the corner dots first. Once you have their rule, ignore them completely and look only at the center shape. Treat the image like three separate, simple puzzles instead of one complex one.
