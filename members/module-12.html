Module 12: Relative Speed

Phase 1: The X-Ray

When two objects move towards each other, the distance between them shrinks at a rate equal to the sum of their speeds.

Entry"Two cars are 100 miles apart. Car A travels at 30mph, Car B at 20mph towards A. How long until they meet?"
Standard"A cyclist leaves London at 10km/h. A runner leaves Brighton (50km away) at 15km/h heading to London. When do they pass?"
Advanced"A train leaves at 4pm at 60mph. Another leaves the destination 300 miles away at 4:30pm at 90mph. At what time do they meet?"

Phase 4: The Saboteur

The "Middle Meet" Trap: Unless both objects are travelling at the exact same speed, they will never meet in the middle.

The Fix: The impact point will always be closer to the starting point of the slower object. Visualizing this stops students from automatically dividing the total distance by 2.
