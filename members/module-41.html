Module 41: Logical Ordering

Phase 1: The X-Ray

Ranking items requires processing relative clues. You must position each item correctly in relation to others, even when the absolute start or end is unknown. The "Ladder" grows as you link the information together.

Advanced Year 2A is taller than B. B is taller than C. Who is shortest?
Average Year 4Monday is before Tuesday. Friday is after Wednesday. Order all four.
Advanced Year 5Sam is heavier than Ben but lighter than Leo. Leo is lighter than Gus. Who is 2nd heaviest?

Phase 4: The Saboteur

The "Absolute" Trap: Assuming a person is "first" just because they are mentioned first in the sentence.

The Fix: Use a **Vertical Line**. As you read a clue like "A is taller than B," draw A above B. Then, if the next clue is "C is taller than A," draw C above A. This physical sketch prevents your memory from "overwriting" the correct relative positions.
