Module 20: Substitution (The Formula Lab)

Phase 1: The X-Ray

Topic Logic: Substitution is the bridge between algebra and arithmetic. We replace general symbols (A, B, C) with specific "ingredients" (values). The complexity lies in recognizing implied operations: $3A$ is $3 \times A$, and $B^2$ is $B \times B$.

EntryIf $a=12$ and $b=5$, find $a - b$.
StandardIf $x=4$ and $y=10$, find $3x + 2y$.
AdvancedIf $p=3$ and $q=5$, find $p^2 + 2(q - 1)$.

Phase 4: The Saboteur

The "Digit Gluing" Trap: If $A=5$, a Type 1 thinker sees $3A$ and thinks it is $35$.

The Fix: In Algebra, the "glue" between a number and a letter is Multiplication. Always imagine a tiny "$\times$" between the coefficient and the variable. $3A$ is $3 \times 5 = 15$.
