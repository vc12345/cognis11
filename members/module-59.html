Module 59: Tense Consistency

Phase 1: The X-Ray

A story should stay on one Timeline. If you start in the Past Tense ("The boy walked"), all subsequent actions must stay in the past. Slipping into Present Tense ("The boy walked to the park and sits down") breaks the logic of time.

Advanced Year 2She played tag and then eats a snack.

Error: Slip from Past to Present.
Fix: "ate a snack."
Average Year 4They arrived late, so they miss the beginning.

Error: Temporal inconsistency.
Fix: "missed the beginning."
Advanced Year 5The researchers noted the change as the chemical reacts with the air.

Error: Slip in descriptive narrative.
Fix: "reacted with the air."

Phase 2: The Anatomy

Parent Instruction: Ask your child to circle every verb in the sentence. If most verbs end in -ed but one ends in -s (e.g. walked vs. walks), that is the "Slip." The first verb usually sets the law for the rest of the sentence.

Phase 3: The Engine

"The explorer climbed the mountain, surveyed the horizon, and sits down to rest."

Identify and fix the temporal slip in the sentence above.

SCANNING VERB SEQUENCE...
> Verb 1: [Climbed] (Past)
> Verb 2: [Surveyed] (Past)
> Verb 3: [Sits] (Present)
> DETECTED: Temporal Anomaly.

Phase 4: The Saboteur

The Trap

The Dialogue Trap. Students often try to fix tense inside a character's speech. For example: He said, "I am happy." The "said" is past, but the "am" is present. This is correct. Only fix tense in the narrative description, not the talking.

The Fix

The Anchor Verb. Identify the very first action in the sentence. This is your Anchor. Every other verb must bow to the Anchor. If the Anchor is past, every verb must be past. If the Anchor is present, every verb must be present.
