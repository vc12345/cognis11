Module 46: Series Progression

Phase 1: The X-Ray

A series is a visual sequence where elements "walk," "pulse," or "rotate" according to fixed rules. To predict the next frame, you must separate the elements and track their movements independently. Usually, there is a Position Rule and a Style Rule (color/shape) happening at the same time.

Advanced Year 2A dot moves one corner clockwise in each frame of a square.
Average Year 4A shape alternates color (Black/White) while moving right.
Advanced Year 5Two elements: One rotates 45°, the other moves 2 steps.

Phase 4: The Saboteur

The Trap: The "Total Image" Trap. Trying to look at the whole frame at once and feeling overwhelmed by the movement.

The Fix: Feature Isolation. Use your finger to cover everything except the one element you are tracking. Find its rule (e.g., "Moves top-left to top-right"), write it down, and then move to the next element. Only combine them at the very end to pick your answer.
