Module 6: Percentage Multipliers

Phase 1: The X-Ray

In the 11+ exam, calculating percentages in multiple slow steps (finding 10%, then doubling, then adding) is a trap. We use Decimal Multipliers to jump straight to the answer. By starting at 1.00 (100%), we simply add or subtract the change. This turns a complex word problem into a single, fast multiplication.

The Discount "A £20 teddy is 10% off. How much do you pay?"
The Bonus "40 marbles plus 20% extra free. What is the new total?"
The Price Hike "A £40 item increases by 15%. Calculate the new price in one step."

Phase 2: The Anatomy

This is Growth vs. Decay. The "Original" bar is always 100% (1.00). An increase stretches past the finish line, while a decrease shrinks back from it. Watch the "Multiplier Bubble" change as the bars move.

Phase 4: The Saboteur

The "Literal Addition" Trap: If a price is £50 and goes up by 10%, students often just add the number 10 (e.g., £50 + 10 = £60). They are treating "%" as a currency, not a ratio!

The Fix: The "Decimal Shortcut." Always turn the percentage into a multiplier first. A 10% increase is 1.10. A 10% decrease is 0.90. This ensures the change is based on the actual starting value.
