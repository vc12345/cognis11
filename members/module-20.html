Phase 1: The X-Ray

Topic Logic: Substitution is the process of replacing symbols (letters or shapes) with their known numerical values. Once the symbols are replaced, the expression becomes a standard calculation that follows **BODMAS** priority.

StandardIf $x = 10$ and $y = 2$, find the value of $x - 3y$.
AdvancedGiven $\triangle = 4$ and $\square = 3$, calculate: $(\triangle \times \square)^2 - \triangle$.

Phase 4: The Saboteur

The "Invisible Multiplier" Trap: In algebra, $3y$ means $3 \times y$. A "Type 1" thinker often forgets this and tries to write the numbers side-by-side (e.g., if $y=2$, they think $3y$ is $32$).

The Fix: Always put brackets around your substitution! $3(y) \rightarrow 3(2) = 6$. This prevents "Digit Gluing" and keeps your operations clear.
