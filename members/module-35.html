Module 35: Word Morphing

Phase 1: The X-Ray

Word Morphing (or "Stolen Letters") tests Structural Lexical Analysis. You are required to move exactly one letter from the first word and insert it into the second word to form two entirely new, valid words. Success relies on identifying which letter's removal leaves a coherent root, while its addition creates a new meaning elsewhere.

Advanced Year 2 "Move one letter from the word CHART to the word EAT to make two new words."
Average Year 4 "One letter can be moved from STEAM to BIT. Identify the letter and its new position."
Advanced Year 5 "Find the letter that moves from POUND to EACH. Both results must be real English words."

Phase 4: The Saboteur

The "One-Sided" Trap: Finding a letter that creates a brilliant new word when added to the second box, but ignoring the fact that the first word is now a gibberish sequence of letters.

The Fix: Use the Dual Dictionary Check. Every time you move a letter, ask yourself: 1. Is the first word still a real word? 2. Is the second word a real word? If either answer is "No," that letter is a decoy. Focus on letters whose removal leaves a common, recognizable root.
