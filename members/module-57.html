Module 57: Cloze (Grammatical)

Phase 1: The X-Ray

Grammatical Cloze logic is about Word Class Syntax. Even if you don't know the story, the words next to the gap tell you what kind of word is needed. An "a" or "the" before a gap usually signals a Noun. A word ending in "-ly" after a gap often signals a Verb.

Advanced Year 2The _____ dog barked loudly.

Position: Before Noun
Slot: Adjective (e.g. Big)
Average Year 4She _____ down the street as fast as she could.

Position: After Subject
Slot: Verb (e.g. Ran)
Advanced Year 5The scientist looked _____ at the petri dish.

Position: After Verb
Slot: Adverb (e.g. Curiously)

Phase 2: The Anatomy

Parent Instruction: Use the "The ____ test." If a word can fit into the sentence "The ____ was big," it is a Noun. If it fits into "I can ____," it is a Verb. Identifying the "Job" of the word is the key.

Phase 4: The Saboteur

The Trap

The Meaning Trap. Students often look for a word that makes a "cool story" but makes no sense grammatically. For example, picking "Bravely" for the gap in "The _____ explorer" because explorers are brave, even though "Bravely" is an adverb and cannot describe a noun.

The Fix

The Slot Test. Forget the story for a second. Look at the words directly before and after the gap. Determiner + ____ + Noun always equals an Adjective. Use the structure as your compass, not the plot.
