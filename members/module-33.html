Module 33: Logical Deduction

Phase 1: The X-Ray

This isn't math—it's **Elimination**. Your goal isn't to find the right answer immediately; it's to cross out everything that is impossible until only the truth remains.

Phase 2: The Anatomy

We use a **Logic Matrix** to keep track of what we know. If Clue A says "The Red shape is not a Square," we put an X in that box.

Phase 3: The Engine

The Mystery: Three friends (Aria, Ben, and Cory) have three different pets (Cat, Dog, Fish).

  • 1. Ben does not have a Cat.
  • 2. Aria has a Fish.
  • 3. Cory does not have a Cat.
Ben's Pet: ?
Aria's Pet: Fish
Cory's Pet: ?

Step 1: Aria has the Fish (given).

Step 2: Ben and Cory don't have the Fish (since Aria has it).

Step 3: Ben and Cory also don't have a Cat (clues 1 & 3).

Conclusion: If Ben doesn't have a Cat or a Fish, he MUST have the Dog!

Phase 4: The Saboteur

The "Assumption" Trap: Guessing an answer because it "seems likely" without having a clue to back it up. In logic, if it isn't proven, it isn't true!
