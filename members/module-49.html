Module 49: Rotated Cubes

Phase 1: The X-Ray

In the 11+ Non-Verbal Reasoning exam, you are often asked to identify a cube after it has been spun or flipped. Success depends on Adjacency Logic. Since you only see three faces at a time, you must pick one "Anchor" face and track its neighbors. If the Star is above the Circle in the original view, it must remain above the Circle in any rotation unless the entire cube has been turned upside down.

Advanced Year 2 "Identify which cube represents a single 90° horizontal turn to the right."
Average Year 4 "Spot the 'Impossible Neighbor'—a face that is known to be opposite but is shown touching."
Advanced Year 5 "Audit orientation: Does the arrow face toward or away from the adjacent Square?"

Phase 4: The Saboteur

The "Impossible Neighbor" Trap: Choosing an answer where two faces are touching, even though you know they are Opposites. Most distractors will use correct symbols but place them in impossible positions.

The Fix: Use the Law of Opposites. If the Star and the Circle are on opposite sides of the cube, they can never be visible at the same time. If a multiple-choice option shows both, cross it out immediately. Don't waste time checking its rotation.
