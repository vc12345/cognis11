Module 22: Missing Lengths

THE "PARALLEL PARTNERS" PRINCIPLE

Phase 1: The X-Ray

A "Type 1" thinker sees a shape and gets overwhelmed by the lines. A "Type 2" thinker only looks at lines going in the same direction. Parallel lines must add up to their partner.

Exam Wording Patterns:
1. "Calculate the perimeter of this L-shaped room."
2. "Side $x$ is missing. Given the total length is 12cm and part is 5cm, find $x$."
3. "Find the missing vertical length in this compound shape."

Phase 2: The Anatomy

In an L-shape, the "Long" side is always equal to the sum of the two "Short" parallel sides.

Vertical Logic: Top Short + Bottom Short = Long Vertical.
Horizontal Logic: Left Short + Right Short = Long Horizontal.

Phase 3: The Engine

Logic: Total Length - Known Part = Missing Length
