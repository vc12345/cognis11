Module 50: Paper Folding

Phase 1: The X-Ray

Paper folding requires Mental Symmetrical Projection. Each fold line acts as a primary axis of reflection. To solve these puzzles, you must reverse the process step-by-step, mirroring every punch across the crease lines until you return to the original sheet dimensions. The core logic is that a hole never "moves"—it only duplicates through reflection.

Advanced Year 2 1 Fold (Half). 1 Punch. Trace the symmetry across a single vertical axis to find the pair.
Average Year 4 2 Folds (Quarter). 2 Punches. Analyze the pattern across both X and Y axes to find the group of 8.
Advanced Year 5 3 Folds (Diagonal or Strip). Complex radial reflection where the hole positions rotate.

Phase 4: The Saboteur

The "Slide" Fallacy: Thinking the hole simply moves to the other side of the paper while staying in the same relative position (e.g. always staying in the "top-left" of its section).

The Fix: Use Crease Anchoring. Every crease is a mirror. If a hole is 10mm away from the fold line on the left, its duplicate MUST be exactly 10mm away on the right. If you have two folds, apply this mirror logic twice—once for each axis. Distance to the crease is the only constant.
